## Public Content

I focus on evaluating the architectural soundness of the proposed dynamic sampling and augmentation pipeline, the fairness of the empirical baseline comparisons, and the systemic complexity introduced by the multi-component framework.

## Overview
Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) has become a dominant paradigm for enhancing the reasoning capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs). However, a pervasive bottleneck in current RLVR recipes (such as GRPO and DAPO) is the reliance on static, offline prompt pools. As the policy evolves during training, its "capability frontier" shifts. Consequently, uniform sampling from a static pool wastes substantial rollout compute on prompts that are either trivially solved (saturated) or impossibly difficult (unreachable). 

To address this, the paper introduces HeaPA, an end-to-end framework designed to dynamically track the learning frontier and organically expand the prompt pool. The system operates on three primary axes: (1) a dual-heap data structure to enforce difficulty-aware "boundary sampling," which isolates medium-difficulty prompts; (2) on-policy query augmentation with asynchronous teacher verification to safely introduce novel prompts without stalling the main RL loop; and (3) lineage-aware pool-statistic re-estimation to prevent highly correlated augmented queries from distorting the curriculum.

## Claimed Contributions
- Identification of the sample inefficiency in standard RLVR static pools and a formulation of boundary sampling to explicitly target the policy's moving capability frontier.
- The design of a Bounded Dual-Heap Prompt Pool (partitioned by a target fraction parameter, $\alpha$) that organically separates scored items into "hard" and "easy" sets while managing unscored items via a cold queue.
- An on-policy query augmentation pipeline that utilizes an asynchronous teacher model to verify newly generated questions, preventing synchronization bottlenecks.
- A lineage graph topology mechanism to dynamically re-estimate pool statistics, counteracting the statistical skew introduced by highly correlated, augmented prompts.
- Empirical validation demonstrating improved sample efficiency and task performance across seven benchmarks, using Qwen2.5-7B on two math reasoning corpora (DAPO-Math and OpenR1-Math), smoothly integrating into both GRPO and DAPO recipes.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The concept of difficulty-aware sampling and curriculum learning is well-established in the broader machine learning literature, and focusing on the "medium-difficulty" or "learnability" frontier has been explored in recent active learning and RLVR works. However, HeaPA distinguishes itself by jointly addressing prompt selection and continuous prompt *generation*. 

The most novel architectural contribution is the lineage-aware pool-statistic re-estimation. When policies autonomously generate variations of prompts, they naturally produce clusters of highly correlated queries. Simply injecting these into a standard replay buffer or prompt pool would lead to mode collapse or oscillatory training dynamics. By maintaining a lineage graph ($G_t$) to track the parent-child relationships of augmented queries and applying topology-aware re-estimation, the authors provide a highly creative and principled solution to a subtle but critical problem in auto-curriculum generation.

## Technical Soundness
The formalization of the RLVR objective (Eq. 1-7) and its adaptation to boundary sampling is rigorous and well-defined. The dual-heap data structure correctly implements the intended logic: maintaining a low partition and a high partition bounded by a target fraction $\alpha$, thereby allowing the system to probabilistically zero in on the boundary. 

Algorithm 1 provides an exceptionally clear mapping of the system's asynchronous nature. Moving the teacher verification step to an asynchronous worker queue (Lines 15-16) is a sound systems-engineering choice. It prevents the notoriously slow generative verification step from blocking the fast policy rollout and update loops. 

However, technical soundness regarding system limits remains an open question. While asynchronous verification hides latency, it does not eliminate the fundamental reliance on a dense teacher model. If the augmentation rate is high, or if the policy explores heavily, the asynchronous queue may either saturate (leading to pool starvation) or incur massive API inference costs. The paper shifts the compute bottleneck rather than entirely eliminating it. 

## Experimental Rigor
The evaluation framework is ambitious, utilizing strong modern baselines (Qwen2.5-7B) and state-of-the-art RLVR recipes (GRPO, DAPO). Evaluating across seven distinct benchmarks provides a robust signal of generalized reasoning improvement within the mathematical domain.

Despite this, there is a severe fatal flaw candidate regarding baseline fairness. HeaPA dynamically expands its training dataset via on-policy augmentation and subsequent teacher verification. If the standard GRPO/DAPO baselines are strictly confined to the static seed dataset ($D_0$), while HeaPA accesses $D_0 \cup D_{aug}$ (where $D_{aug}$ contains novel, teacher-verified queries), the experimental design is fundamentally confounded. In this scenario, it is impossible to determine whether the performance gains stem from the sophisticated boundary sampling and lineage tracking, or simply from training on a larger volume of teacher-annotated data. 

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Highly Impactful Problem Space**: Addressing the sample inefficiency of RLVR rollouts is arguably one of the most pressing challenges in post-training reasoning models today.
- **Architectural Elegance**: The integration of the dual-heap system, the cold queue, and the lineage graph (visually summarized effectively in Figure 1 and Algorithm 1) demonstrates deep system-level thinking and structural innovation.
- **Compatibility**: The method is optimizer-agnostic and serves as a plug-and-play enhancement for existing group-based RLVR recipes, increasing its potential adoption in the community.
- **Clarity of Presentation**: The complex, asynchronous multi-worker pipeline is documented with high clarity, utilizing precise mathematical notation in the preliminaries and method sections.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Confounded Experimental Baselines (Fatal Flaw Candidate)**: As noted, if baselines do not have access to an equivalently sized pool of teacher-verified prompts, the claims of algorithmic superiority are unsupported. The performance gains may trivially reduce to "more data yields better results."
- **Systemic Complexity and Hyperparameter Sensitivity**: The framework introduces a multitude of interacting mechanisms. The text introduces parameters such as the pool capacity $N$, the low partition target fraction $\alpha$, and the augmentation batch sizes. Without rigorous component-wise ablations, the necessity of the heavy engineering (specifically the lineage tracking) over simpler dynamic buffers is questionable.
- **Domain Overfitting (Math Reliance)**: The evaluation relies exclusively on exact-match mathematical reasoning (DAPO-Math, OpenR1-Math). It is uncertain how the dual-heap boundary logic and the teacher verification scale to domains with noisier, programmatic, or subjective verifiers (e.g., code generation or open-ended instruction following).
- **Area Chair Verification Risk**: The abstract and introduction include a direct GitHub repository link (`https://github.com/horizon-rl/HeaPA`). Depending on the venue's exact double-blind policy, and whether the `horizon-rl` organization or its commit history contains deanonymizing information, this poses a risk of desk-rejection or policy violation. This must be independently verified by the Area Chair.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
- **Data-Controlled Baselines**: To isolate the effect of the sampling and curriculum strategy, HeaPA must be compared against a GRPO/DAPO baseline that is trained on a static, offline dataset equal in size and composition to the *final* augmented pool used by HeaPA.
- **Component-Wise Ablation Studies**: Empirical results showing the performance degradation when individually disabling (1) lineage-aware re-estimation (treating augmented queries as independent), (2) boundary sampling (reverting to uniform sampling over the augmented pool), and (3) on-policy augmentation.
- **Sensitivity Analyses**: Sweeps over the critical hyperparameter $\alpha$ (to demonstrate robustness to the definition of the "boundary") and the total pool capacity $N$.
- **Computational Overhead Profiling**: A detailed wall-clock breakdown comparing the rollout savings against the additional inference cost (FLOPs/API calls) incurred by the asynchronous teacher verification and the continuous updating of the lineage graph topology $G_t$.

## What Would Change My Assessment
My assessment would shift significantly toward a strong accept if the authors can demonstrate that HeaPA outperforms baselines even when those baselines are granted access to the exact same total set of teacher-verified prompts (e.g., an offline pre-augmented dataset). Furthermore, providing the missing ablation studies to prove that the lineage graph re-estimation is strictly necessary to prevent training instability would justify the system's complexity. A brief discussion or experiment demonstrating applicability outside of pure mathematics (e.g., code generation) would also elevate the paper's significance. Finally, confirmation that the GitHub repository adheres to double-blind anonymity rules is required.

## Provisional Leaning
Lean Accept. The paper introduces a highly creative and well-engineered solution to a pressing efficiency bottleneck in LLM reasoning. The lineage tracking for augmented prompts is a particularly insightful contribution to automated curriculum generation. However, the overall recommendation is heavily tempered by the risk of confounded baselines and high systemic complexity. If the baselines are proven mathematically fair and proper ablations are provided, this represents a significant and highly citable contribution to the RLVR literature.
