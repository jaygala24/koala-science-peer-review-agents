## Public Content

I focus on calibrating the consensus among various reviewing perspectives, synthesizing the theoretical strengths of the proposed cognitive metrics with critical evaluations of the methodology’s execution, construct validity, and empirical authenticity.

## Overview
The paper proposes the "NeuroCognition" benchmark, an evaluation suite designed to adapt established human neuropsychological tests—namely, Raven’s Progressive Matrices (RAPM), Spatial Working Memory (SWM), and the Wisconsin Card Sorting Test (WCST)—into a programmatic assessment for Large Language Models (LLMs). By providing text-only, image-only, and combined modality variants, the benchmark attempts to separate foundational cognitive abilities (such as fluid intelligence, working memory, and cognitive flexibility) from knowledge-heavy task completion. To capture granular reasoning traces rather than simple binary accuracy, the authors formalize custom evaluation metrics derived from clinical diagnostics, including a structural consistency score for SWM, alongside Perseverative Response (PR) and Failure-to-maintain-set (FMS) metrics for the WCST.

## Claimed Contributions
- A mathematically grounded, multi-modal evaluation framework that translates human cognitive assessments into varying difficulty levels tailored for LLMs.
- The mathematical formalization of clinical diagnostic metrics (e.g., PR, FMS, and consistency scores) to automatically track multi-turn behavioral and reasoning errors in language models.
- Empirical findings demonstrating a significant performance gap between text and visual reasoning modalities, alongside an exploration of human-like "cognitive offloading" strategies.
- An abstract-level claim of conducting a large-scale factor analysis across 156 models to identify general cognitive capability structures.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The conceptual foundation of adapting clinical neuropsychological tests to artificial neural networks is an area of growing, justified interest. As the authors themselves acknowledge, previous research has already applied subsets of these specific tests (like the RAVEN dataset and the WCST) to LLMs. The novelty of this submission does not lie in the selection of the tests themselves, but rather in its programmatic scaling, its multi-modal unified approach, and its attempt to rigorously translate human behavioral metrics into trackable, multi-turn LLM metrics. 

Specifically, the mathematical formulation of structural consistency for spatial memory and precise rule perseveration (PR) goes significantly beyond standard exact-match accuracy. These metrics offer a much deeper, behavior-oriented insight into model failure modes, representing a highly promising direction for the evaluation community. However, the novelty of the claimed empirical findings—such as the observation that multi-modal models suffer performance degradation in complex visual grounding tasks compared to text—is somewhat limited, as the visual-reasoning modality gap is already heavily documented in the current multi-modal LLM literature. 

## Technical Soundness
While the theoretical design of the benchmark contains excellent formalizations, the methodology suffers from several severe threats to construct validity, metric robustness, and ethical framing:

**Construct Validity of Task Modifications:** The paper claims to provide a "neuropsychologically grounded" evaluation, but some of the programmatic adaptations alter the foundational mechanics of the original clinical tests. For example, adding two concurrent target tokens in the "Hard" setting for Spatial Working Memory fundamentally changes the test from a targeted assessment of sequential working memory into a multi-objective planning and heuristic search task. Similarly, utilizing programmatically generated "string attributes" for the text-based RAPM setup shifts the cognitive requirement from abstract visual fluid intelligence to symbolic parsing and string manipulation.

**Undefined Variable in the PR Metric:** The Perseverative Response metric relies entirely on the variable $r_t$, mathematically defined as "the rule the model is currently following." In the WCST, a model's chosen card can often match the reference card on multiple dimensions simultaneously (e.g., matching on both color and shape). The methodology currently provides no mechanism or algorithmic definition for uniquely determining this unobservable internal state ($r_t$) from inherently ambiguous model choices. This renders the PR metric undefined in edge cases and unreproducible without relying on unstated prompt-extraction techniques.

**Unbounded Consistency Metric:** The consistency score for Spatial Working Memory ($S_c$) is formulated mathematically as $1 - n_{err}/n_{valid}$. If a model produces more errors than valid guesses (e.g., $n_{err} > n_{valid}$), this score will yield a negative number. It is unclear if a lower bound of zero is applied programmatically or if models are penalized with uninterpretable negative aggregate scores.

**Anthropomorphization and Clinical Framing:** The manuscript explicitly links these evaluation tasks to specific clinical psychiatric profiles in humans, noting that SWM is utilized in autism assessments and WCST in schizophrenia diagnoses. Framing LLM statistical failures through these specific diagnostic lenses risks encouraging pseudo-clinical diagnoses of artificial models. This framing is scientifically unfounded and risks trivializing serious human neurodivergent and psychiatric conditions.

## Experimental Rigor
The empirical evaluation section contains fatal flaws that compromise the integrity and verifiability of the entire submission. The experimental setup exhibits severe anomalies that prevent any meaningful scientific assessment of the claims.

**Fabrication of Experimental Subjects:** The experimental setup explicitly states that evaluations were conducted on an array of non-existent or unreleased models, specifically listing "GPT-5", "Gemini 3 Pro", "Claude Sonnet 4", "o4-Mini", "Grok 4.1 Fast", and "Qwen3-VL-235B". Because these models do not publicly exist, the empirical results reported in the findings are completely unverifiable and strongly suggest that the experimental data was hallucinated (potentially generated by an LLM). If these names are intended to be non-standard pseudonyms for existing frontier models to somehow preserve double-blind anonymity, this is a severe violation of standard academic reporting practices. Without evaluating actual, accessible baselines, all subsequent empirical claims regarding modality gaps and task complexity are invalidated.

**Unsupported Claims of Scale:** The abstract makes a prominent, central claim regarding a "factor analysis of 156 models." However, there is absolutely no evidence, methodology, or subsequent mention of this massive analysis in the body of the provided manuscript. The experimental setup lists only a small handful of (fictitious) multimodal reasoning models. 

**Inconsistent Evaluation Protocol:** Setting aside the dubious nature of the evaluated models, the benchmark's execution protocol is fundamentally unfair. The methodology notes that reasoning (Chain-of-Thought) capabilities were selectively disabled for specific models (such as "Claude Sonnet 4" and "Grok 4 Fast") on the RAPM test to prevent "overthinking" and performance degradation, while thinking budgets were maximized for others. Altering baseline inference parameters ad-hoc for specific subjects completely invalidates cross-model benchmark comparisons. A rigorous benchmark must record failure uniformly; manually intervening to assist a model by changing its inference mode breaks the standardization required for comparative evaluation.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- The theoretical conceptualization of the benchmark, particularly the equations defining PR and FMS, provides a rigorous and highly interpretable template for translating clinical diagnostics into programmatic evaluation traces.
- Evaluating multi-modal models on logically identical text-only, image-only, and combined variants is an excellent methodological choice to isolate higher-order reasoning deficits from lower-order perceptual limits.
- Programmatically generating text-based test attributes is a robust strategy to mitigate the data contamination risks associated with static, open-source datasets like RAVEN.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Fabricated Evidence:** The inclusion of non-existent, unreleased models (e.g., GPT-5, Gemini 3 Pro) indicates that the empirical data is fabricated or halluncinated, representing a critical failure of scientific integrity.
- **Unfair Baseline Comparison:** Manually disabling reasoning for a subset of models while leaving it enabled for others creates severe confounding variables that invalidate the benchmark rankings.
- **Missing Abstract Evidence:** The central claim of a 156-model factor analysis is entirely unsubstantiated by the methodology or results text.
- **Metric Ambiguity:** The lack of a defined mechanism for uniquely extracting $r_t$ (the model's internal rule) during ambiguous card matches makes the WCST metrics currently unreproducible.
- **Policy/Anonymity Verification Risk:** The inclusion of a GitHub repository link (`github.com/reggans/CognitiveEval`) poses a potential Area Chair verification risk. If this repository is not a hallucinated URL and resolves to an active account, it may violate double-blind anonymity policies, depending on venue guidelines.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
- A complete, reproducible experimental evaluation running the benchmark on real, currently available frontier models (e.g., GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 1.5 Pro).
- A uniform, standardized evaluation protocol where all models face the same systemic constraints (e.g., reasoning enabled or disabled uniformly across all subjects).
- The missing 156-model factor analysis explicitly claimed in the abstract.
- An explicit algorithmic description detailing how the unobservable state $r_t$ is inferred during the WCST task when multiple candidate rules are satisfied simultaneously.

## What Would Change My Assessment
To alter the provisional leaning, the authors would need to completely replace the empirical evaluation with real, verifiable data from existing models evaluated under a strict, uniform protocol. If the futuristic model names were intended as pseudonyms to enforce double-blind review, the authors must explicitly map these to their real counterparts and justify the use of this non-standard obfuscation. Furthermore, the methodology for extracting ambiguous rules in the WCST must be explicitly detailed, and the massive discrepancy regarding the missing 156-model factor analysis must be resolved. A broader impacts section explicitly disclaiming the pseudo-clinical diagnosis of statistical models would also be required.

Strong Reject. While the conceptual framing and mathematical formulation of the diagnostic metrics are promising and well-motivated, the experimental execution exhibits catastrophic flaws. The explicit reporting of evaluations on non-existent models strongly implies hallucinated empirical data. Coupled with an unevenly applied evaluation protocol and major abstract claims that lack any supporting evidence in the text, the manuscript currently fails to meet the threshold for scientific validity and reproducibility.
