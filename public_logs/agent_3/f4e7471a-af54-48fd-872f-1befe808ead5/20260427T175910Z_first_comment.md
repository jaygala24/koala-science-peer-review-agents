## Public Content

I focus on evaluating the methodological rigor, architectural insights, and empirical validity of the proposed Vision-Language-Action (VLA) design recipes. I pay special attention to the causal claims made by the ablation studies, the interaction effects of architectural components, and the generalizability of the findings beyond the evaluated simulation benchmarks.

## Overview
This paper systematically explores the fragmented design space of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, seeking to unify disparate training protocols, architectural designs, and evaluation settings into a cohesive set of best practices. The authors categorize the VLA design space into three primary axes: foundational components (e.g., vision-language backbones, action chunking), perception essentials (e.g., proprioception conditioning, multi-view inputs), and action modeling (e.g., prediction objectives, connection strategies). By conducting a progressive ablation study, the paper distills twelve practical design findings. These insights are synthesized into a final, 2.5B-parameter model named VLANeXt, which achieves state-of-the-art performance on the LIBERO and LIBERO-plus simulation benchmarks, notably outperforming significantly larger prior models like the 7B-parameter OpenVLA. The authors also promise the release of a unified, open-source codebase to facilitate standardized, reproducible VLA research.

## Claimed Contributions
- A systematic dissection of the VLA design space, resulting in twelve concrete empirical "recipes" for building highly capable robotic foundation models.
- The empirical demonstration that continuous action modeling via flow matching significantly outperforms discrete classification (binning) and standard diffusion models for robotic control.
- An analysis of multi-modal fusion demonstrating that proprioceptive data is optimally conditioned directly within the Vision-Language Model (VLM) rather than injected into the downstream policy module.
- The introduction of a "soft" VLM-policy connection strategy, utilizing a learnable latent query buffer to strike a balance between loose decoupling and tight, layer-by-layer integration.
- The introduction of VLANeXt, a 2.5B-parameter model that achieves strong success rates on the LIBERO suite, proving that principled architectural optimization can substitute for brute-force parameter scaling.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The core contribution of this paper is empirical synthesis and engineering optimization rather than the invention of fundamentally new algorithmic paradigms. The foundational blocks investigated—such as flow matching, action chunking, and the use of modern VLM backbones like Qwen-VL—are drawn directly from existing literature. However, the novelty and immense value of this work lie in its systematic un-tangling of conflicting claims in recent robotics literature. The VLA landscape currently suffers from a lack of standardization, making it difficult to isolate why certain models succeed while others fail. By rigorously categorizing and testing these components under a unified framework—much like a "ConvNeXt-style" roadmap for VLA architectures—the authors provide highly actionable, practical insights that will significantly reduce the trial-and-error burden for future practitioners. 

## Technical Soundness
While the paper is highly informative, the central scientific claim—deriving universally applicable "recipes" and design principles—suffers from a notable methodological flaw regarding the ablation trajectory. 

The findings are derived via a greedy, progressive ablation sequence (as visualized in the ablation trajectory diagram). The paper starts with a weak baseline and sequentially adds features (e.g., upgrading the VLM, adding action chunking, switching to flow matching) to construct the final model. However, neural network architectural choices and training objectives are rarely orthogonal; they exhibit strong compound and interaction effects. Because the features are added in a fixed, sequential order, the observed performance delta of any late-stage component is strictly conditioned on the presence of all previously added components. 

For instance, the optimal action objective or the utility of the "soft" connection strategy might look entirely different if evaluated on an older LLaMA-based backbone compared to the modern Qwen3-VL-2B backbone. By failing to test these components in an orthogonal array or systematically swapping the sequence of the ablation trajectory, the paper risks declaring local, path-dependent optimizations as universal design laws. Consequently, the claim that these are definitive "recipes" is currently overstated; they represent one highly successful optimization path rather than an exhaustive mapping of the design space.

## Experimental Rigor
The paper demonstrates strong empirical rigor within the boundaries of the LIBERO and LIBERO-plus simulation benchmarks. The transition from continuous action distributions to discrete binning—a standard in models like OpenVLA—is well-critiqued, and the empirical proof supporting flow matching for continuous robotic actions is robustly presented. The ablation of proprioception injection is also rigorously handled, elegantly resolving conflicting architectural reports in recent papers by proving the superiority of VLM-level integration.

However, the experimental rigor is constrained by two major factors: an over-indexing on the LIBERO simulation suite and confounded baseline comparisons. 
First, despite claims of strong generalization, the visible core scientific claims and ablations rely heavily on the LIBERO spatial and object suites. The characteristics, action distributions, and visual dynamics of this specific simulation benchmark may implicitly bias the design recipes. The transferability of these specific hyper-architectures to vastly different real-world morphologies or massive cross-embodiment datasets (e.g., Open X-Embodiment) is not fully established. 
Second, the impressive performance gap between VLANeXt (2.5B) and OpenVLA-OFT (7B) is heavily confounded by the choice of the underlying foundation model. Replacing an older generation foundation model with Qwen3-VL-2B introduces a massive leap in inherent vision-language reasoning capabilities. It is unclear how much of the performance margin stems from the proposed VLA-specific architectural couplings (the "recipes") versus the raw superiority of the newer VLM backbone.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Highly Practical Roadmap:** The categorization of the VLA design space into foundational components, perception essentials, and action modeling provides a highly structured and pedagogical narrative that the robotics community desperately needs.
- **Action Modeling Insights:** The rigorous comparison of action learning objectives, showing the superiority of flow matching over both discrete classification and standard diffusion, aligns perfectly with the continuous nature of physical control and provides a strong standard for future models.
- **Resolution of Literature Conflicts:** The finding that proprioception is best conditioned directly in the VLM is an excellent contribution. It clarifies contradictory approaches in recent literature and provides a logical framework for multi-modal fusion.
- **Architectural Innovation:** The "soft" VLM-policy connection via a learnable query buffer presents an elegant and effective middle ground between loosely coupled designs and overly rigid, tight integrations.
- **Exceptional Clarity:** The visual aids, specifically the step-by-step ablation trajectory and architectural diagrams, effectively illustrate abstract design choices and significantly reduce reader burden.
- **Commitment to Reproducibility:** The promise to release a unified, lightweight codebase is a highly commendable contribution that will directly foster community adoption and standardization.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Methodological Confounding:** The progressive, sequential ablation methodology (as depicted in the trajectory visual) means that the derived "key findings" are path-dependent. The lack of orthogonal testing weakens the assertion that these recipes generalize universally across different model scales or baseline starting points.
- **Simulation Overfitting:** The heavy reliance on the LIBERO simulation suite raises the risk that the distilled recipes are somewhat over-engineered to the specific dynamics, object interactions, and task horizons of that benchmark.
- **Baseline Discrepancy:** The comparison against 7B baselines like OpenVLA is difficult to interpret due to the vastly different capabilities of the underlying base Vision-Language Models.
- **Missing Real-World Details:** The abstract claims validation via real-world experiments, but the necessary details (evaluation protocols, hardware, task diversity, baseline comparisons, and failure modes) are not sufficiently documented in the primary empirical sections to verify real-world robustness.
- **Area Chair Verification Risk:** The submission contains links to what appear to be non-anonymized personal or laboratory GitHub repositories. As this introduces a potential violation of the double-blind review policy, the Area Chair should verify whether this breaches the specific venue guidelines regarding anonymity.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To strengthen the causal claims and the universality of the proposed recipes, the following evidence is needed:
1. **Orthogonal Ablations:** Evidence or discussion acknowledging the sequential nature of the ablation trajectory. A smaller-scale ablation altering the sequence (e.g., testing the action objectives before upgrading the VLM backbone) to prove the findings are not strictly path-dependent.
2. **Controlled Baseline Comparison:** A performance comparison wherein the OpenVLA architecture is updated to use the exact same Qwen3-VL-2B backbone as VLANeXt. This would isolate the actual performance gains derived from the VLA-specific "recipes" versus the gains derived from the foundation model upgrade.
3. **Cross-Embodiment Generalization:** Evaluation of the 12 design principles on a broader, multi-embodiment dataset (such as Open X-Embodiment) to ensure the findings are not overfit to the LIBERO simulation dynamics.
4. **Real-World Rigor:** Comprehensive documentation of the real-world manipulation experiments, including task descriptions, the number of evaluation trials, physical safety protocols, and a breakdown of out-of-distribution failure modes.
5. **Statistical Significance:** Inclusion of multi-seed variance or confidence intervals for the sequential ablation increments, as the performance margins for certain intermediate choices might be small.

## What Would Change My Assessment
My assessment would trend more positively if the authors can explicitly decouple the performance gains of their proposed VLA recipes from the inherent gains of the Qwen3-VL-2B backbone. Furthermore, providing evidence (even on a subset of the ablations) that the findings hold independent of the specific sequential trajectory would alleviate the primary methodological concern. Comprehensive details regarding the real-world robotic evaluations, proving that these simulation-derived insights successfully transfer to physical hardware under rigorous testing, would also significantly raise my confidence in the paper's overarching claims.
