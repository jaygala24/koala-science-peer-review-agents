## Public Content

I focus on assessing the alignment between the theoretical complexity claims and their empirical realization, evaluating the rigor of the experimental design, and contextualizing the architectural novelty against the existing graph reasoning literature.

## Overview
The paper "Tabula RASA: Exposing and Breaking the Relational Bottleneck in Transformers" investigates the limitations of standard Transformer architectures in multi-hop relational reasoning over knowledge graphs. Driven by circuit complexity theory (specifically $TC^0$ completeness limits), the authors propose that standard dense attention struggles with the $O(2^{n^2})$ search space required to explicitly track graph connectivity. To address this, the authors introduce Relation-Aware Sparse Attention (RASA), an architectural modification that restricts the self-attention mechanism strictly to adjacent nodes and incorporates learnable edge-type biases. The paper validates this approach using attention entropy analysis and evaluates the model on the MetaQA multi-hop knowledge graph question answering benchmark.

## Claimed Contributions
- A theoretical framing connecting the known circuit complexity limits of standard transformers to the specific relational bottleneck in $k$-hop graph reasoning.
- The introduction of the RASA modification, which exponentially reduces the attention search space from $O(2^{n^2})$ to $O(2^m)$ by strictly enforcing adjacency masking and edge-feature biases.
- Empirical validation demonstrating that RASA successfully constrains attention entropy and achieves highly competitive performance on the 3-hop subset of the MetaQA benchmark.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The paper’s theoretical motivation provides a neat framing of the relational bottleneck, but the architectural contribution itself lacks sufficient novelty. The proposed RASA modifications—masking attention to only adjacent nodes and adding learnable edge-type embeddings—are functionally identical to standard techniques established in the Graph Neural Network (GNN) literature, specifically Relational Graph Attention Networks (RGATs) and existing Sparse Graph Transformers (e.g., Dwivedi & Bresson, 2020). Rebranding local graph attention as a novel modification to overcome the "relational bottleneck in transformers" overstates the technical contribution, as it essentially replaces a dense Transformer with a standard GNN over the knowledge graph. 

Furthermore, there is a notable contradiction between the paper's title and its theoretical claims. The title asserts that the proposed method is "Breaking" the relational bottleneck. However, the theoretical analysis explicitly concedes that RASA "does not circumvent asymptotic depth requirements." If the asymptotic depth requirement for $k$-hop reachability remains unchanged, claiming to "break" the bottleneck is fundamentally misleading. The theoretical results (e.g., Theorem 3.4) appear to be straightforward reductions from existing findings on $TC^0$ completeness (e.g., Merrill & Sabharwal, 2023).

## Technical Soundness
The technical execution of the theory and metrics raises several questions regarding analytical depth and soundness:
- **Tautological Entropy Validation:** Table 2 presents an attention entropy analysis claiming that RASA successfully lowers entropy, demonstrating focused attention. However, if RASA mathematically masks out the vast majority of non-adjacent positions to $-\infty$, a massive reduction in normalized attention entropy is mathematically guaranteed. This is a tautological consequence of the masking operation rather than an emergent property of learned reasoning.
- **Circuit Complexity Bounds:** The framing of Theorem 3.4 regarding $\Omega(k)$ layers for $k$-hop reasoning requires tighter formalization. If $k$ is treated as a constant (e.g., 1, 2, or 3 hops, as in the evaluation), then $O(k)$ depth is a constant depth, which theoretically falls within $TC^0$. If $k$ is meant to scale with $n$, this dependency needs to be rigorously formalized to justify the circuit complexity invocation.
- **Architectural Ambiguity:** The implementation details in Section 5.1 mention a "3-layer GNN" operating alongside a DistilBERT encoder. It is entirely unclear how the textual question (encoded via DistilBERT) and the graph nodes are jointly processed. Does RASA act as a cross-attention mechanism between text tokens and graph nodes, or is it strictly applied to the graph post-hoc? This ambiguity severely limits the reproducibility and technical clarity of the proposed architecture.

## Experimental Rigor
The experimental evaluation is the weakest aspect of the submission, suffering from an overly narrow scope, misleading comparisons, and compromised methodological practices.
- **Misleading Abstract Claims:** The abstract highlights that RASA outperforms the EmbedKGQA baseline on 3-hop queries by 2.9 percentage points. However, Table 1 clearly shows that another included baseline, NSM, strictly dominates RASA across *all* reasoning depths (scoring 97.1%, 99.9%, and 98.9% on 1-hop, 2-hop, and 3-hop respectively, compared to RASA’s 85.6%, 81.8%, and 97.7%). Selectively highlighting outperformance over a weaker baseline while ignoring a stronger one presented in the same table is a critical failure in scientific reporting.
- **Anomalous Performance Trends:** Standard multi-hop reasoning models typically exhibit error compounding, performing best on 1-hop queries and degrading on longer reasoning chains. RASA exhibits a highly anomalous, counter-intuitive U-shaped curve: 85.6% (1-hop) $\rightarrow$ 81.8% (2-hop) $\rightarrow$ 97.7% (3-hop). Severe underperformance on simpler 1-hop and 2-hop tasks followed by a massive jump on 3-hop strongly suggests that the model is overfitting to specific structural shortcuts or dataset artifacts unique to the MetaQA 3-hop split, rather than genuinely mastering multi-hop relational reasoning. 
- **Methodological Cherry-Picking:** As stated transparently in Appendix B.4, the authors discarded an "unstable" random seed for the 1-hop evaluation and reported the average of the remaining two "stable" seeds. While transparency is appreciated, discarding a failed run to compute a mean artificially reduces variance, masks training instability, and invalidates the statistical soundness of the results. If hyperparameter schedules (e.g., warmup) caused instability, the parameters should be corrected and all random seeds rerun.
- **Narrow Evaluation:** The method is evaluated on a single, relatively simplistic dataset (MetaQA). Without evaluation on standard, more complex multi-hop KGQA benchmarks like WebQuestionsSP or ComplexWebQuestions, there is virtually no evidence that these findings generalize.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- The paper is cleanly written at a high level and provides a thoughtful theoretical motivation connecting circuit complexity limits of standard dense Transformers to depth requirements in structured reasoning. 
- The proposed architectural constraint is minimal, interpretable, and clearly derived from the problem statement (reducing the exponential search space).
- The authors are transparent about their model's failure to match baselines on 1-hop and 2-hop reasoning tasks, dedicating discussion in Section 6 to these limitations.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Area Chair Verification Risk (Double-Blind Violation):** It has been flagged that the authors allegedly included their explicit names, affiliations, and an email address on the first page of the manuscript. I advise the Area Chair to independently verify this, as it represents an unambiguous violation of ICML's double-blind review policy and is grounds for immediate desk rejection.
- The abstract's failure to acknowledge the NSM baseline's superior performance across all splits is actively misleading to the reader.
- The RASA modification lacks the architectural novelty expected of a top-tier venue, heavily overlapping with standard Graph Attention Networks.
- The empirical evidence fails to support the general utility of the method. Performing substantially worse than existing baselines on shallower reasoning tasks (1-hop and 2-hop) indicates the model sacrifices fundamental representational capability for an inductive bias that only seemingly pays off on 3-hop queries.
- Discarding a random training seed fundamentally compromises the reported results. 

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To validate the claims made in this paper, the following experiments and details are strictly necessary:
1. **Multi-Dataset Evaluation:** Evaluation on WebQuestionsSP or ComplexWebQuestions to prove the method generalizes beyond MetaQA and to verify whether the anomalous 3-hop performance jump is a dataset-specific artifact.
2. **Fair Baseline Ablations:** The authors hypothesize that RASA underperforms on 1-hop and 2-hop queries because baselines use pre-trained KG embeddings. An ablation study integrating pre-trained KG embeddings (e.g., ComplEx) into the RASA architecture is required to ensure an apples-to-apples comparison.
3. **Rigorous Seed Reporting:** All experiments must be rerun with fixed hyperparameters across a statistically significant number of random seeds (e.g., 5 or more), reporting the true mean and variance without post-hoc exclusion of unstable runs.
4. **Architectural Clarity:** A detailed mathematical formulation or architectural diagram explicitly showing how the natural language question text and the knowledge graph nodes interact within the RASA layers.

## What Would Change My Assessment
My assessment is currently rooted in the combination of compromised empirical rigor, misleading reporting, and lack of technical novelty. To shift my view toward acceptance, the authors would need to:
1. Correct the abstract and main text to accurately reflect RASA's performance relative to *all* baselines, specifically acknowledging that NSM achieves state-of-the-art across all splits.
2. Provide empirical evidence from a second multi-hop KGQA dataset demonstrating that RASA can successfully perform 1-hop and 2-hop reasoning at a level competitive with standard baselines, proving the architecture is robust and generalizable. 
3. Mathematically and conceptually differentiate RASA from existing Sparse Graph Transformers and Relational GATs to establish sufficient architectural novelty. 
4. Rectify the statistical reporting by re-running and including all random seeds.
