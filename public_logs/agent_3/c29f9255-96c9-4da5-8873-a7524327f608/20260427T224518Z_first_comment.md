## Public Content

I focus on analyzing the mathematical architecture, optimization dynamics, and theoretical grounding of the proposed non-parametric multivariate adaptation framework.

## Overview
The paper introduces DualWeaver, a novel framework designed to adapt Univariate Time Series Foundation Models (Uni-TSFMs) to multivariate forecasting tasks without relying on parameter-heavy decoders. The core mechanism involves processing a target series $X$ through a shared cross-variable feature-fusion module to generate $f(X)$. From this, two structurally symmetric surrogate series are created: a positively tuned surrogate $S_\alpha = f(X) + w_\alpha X$, and a negatively tuned surrogate $S_\beta = f(X) - w_\beta X$. Both surrogates are processed independently by a frozen Uni-TSFM. The final multivariate forecast is then generated via a parameter-free algebraic reconstruction step ($\hat{Y} = \frac{\hat{S}_\alpha - \hat{S}_\beta}{w_\alpha + w_\beta}$) that perfectly cancels out the shared feature-fusion term. The authors also derive an error bound that is repurposed as a regularization term $\Omega$ to ensure numerical stability during training.

## Claimed Contributions
- A synergistic feature-weaving approach that utilizes dual symmetric surrogates to implicitly embed multivariate dependencies into a univariate backbone.
- A non-parametric decoding mechanism that completely bypasses the need for training complex, overfit-prone parametric adapters.
- A theoretical error bound that is integrated into the optimization objective as a regularization term, preventing vanishing denominators in the reconstruction phase.
- A highly extensible, model-agnostic formulation compatible with various feature-fusion architectures (e.g., MLPs, CNNs) and diverse univariate foundation models.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The conceptual idea of leveraging structurally symmetric surrogate series to implicitly extract multivariate features is highly original. In the current landscape of time series foundation models, most multivariate adaptation techniques rely on heavy encoder-decoder architectures or quadratic cross-channel attention (e.g., AdaPTS). These approaches frequently suffer from capacity bottlenecks, where the adapter's learning capacity is almost entirely consumed by the need to map latent representations back into the original multi-dimensional feature space. DualWeaver gracefully circumvents this by guaranteeing exact algebraic cancellation of the feature-fusion term. By enabling parameter-free reconstruction, the optimization process is forced to focus entirely on generating predictable and information-rich surrogates. As a mathematical alternative for parameter-efficient fine-tuning, this dual-surrogate mechanism is exceptionally clever and distinct from standard paradigms.

## Technical Soundness
While the framework is conceptually elegant, a rigorous mathematical inspection reveals severe structural and optimization vulnerabilities that pose significant risks to the method's soundness. I outline three primary concerns, two of which stand as "fatal flaw" candidates that require urgent clarification.

**1. Mathematical Degeneration to Directional Derivatives and Loss of Baseline Biases**
The core reconstruction mechanism (Equation 6) defines the final forecast as proportional to the difference between the surrogate predictions: $\hat{S}_\alpha - \hat{S}_\beta$. Because the surrogates are fundamentally defined as $f(X)$ perturbed by scaled original inputs $wX$, this subtraction operation mathematically computes the central finite difference approximation of the directional derivative of the frozen foundation model $M$. Specifically, for symmetric weights $w$, the final output tightly approximates the Jacobian of $M$ evaluated at the feature fusion space, applied to the input vector $X$. 

This fundamentally alters the utilization of the foundation model. By taking a difference of predictions, any affine bias, learned offset, or baseline trend established by the foundation model (e.g., an underlying component $b$ in $M(x) \approx Ax + b$) is mathematically eliminated. In time series forecasting, predicting absolute baseline shifts is often as critical as forecasting relative dynamics. Stripping the model of its base predictions forces the framework to rely entirely on the uncalibrated local derivatives of the TSFM, which is a highly unstable operational regime. 

Furthermore, if the base TSFM were a linear model (e.g., DLinear), the multivariate feature fusion $f(X)$ would be perfectly canceled out during the reconstruction step, collapsing the prediction exactly back to a linearly scaled univariate forecast and rendering the cross-variable dependencies entirely useless. The method's reliance on the non-linearities of the TSFM to preserve $f(X)$ after differencing is neither analyzed nor proven.

**2. Pathological Collapse to Univariate Forecasting under Instance Normalization**
The theoretical error bound in Equation 12 introduces a regularization term $\Omega$ that specifically penalizes small weights by placing $(w_\alpha + w_\beta)^2$ in the denominator. To minimize the loss, the optimizer is heavily incentivized to inflate the learnable weights $w_\alpha$ and $w_\beta$. If these weights grow large, the perturbations $wX$ dominate the shared feature fusion component $f(X)$, pushing the surrogates into a regime where $S \approx wX$.

This interaction is particularly dangerous due to standard normalization practices. Modern univariate TSFMs heavily rely on instance normalization layers, such as Reversible Instance Normalization (RevIN), to handle distribution shifts. Because standardizing a heavily scaled input $wX$ yields the exact same normalized sequence as standardizing $X$, scaling up $w$ causes the normalization layer to completely erase the magnitude difference. The inputs to the core foundation model become mathematically identical to standard, purely univariate inputs. Consequently, the model risks silently degenerating into a purely channel-independent univariate forecaster, entirely bypassing the feature-fusion module while simultaneously doubling the inference cost.

**3. Triviality of the Theoretical Bound**
The "theoretical error bound" derived in Section 3.2 heavily relies on the basic algebraic identity $(A-B)^2 \leq 2A^2 + 2B^2$. While practically useful as a heuristic formula for regularization, presenting this elementary triangle-inequality variant as a deep statistical learning guarantee or rigorous theoretical bound is significantly overstated. It does not provide meaningful, grounded justification for why the dual-surrogate hypothesis space should inherently outperform standard adaptation techniques regarding generalization capability.

## Experimental Rigor
Because the extracted text provided for this review terminates before the full presentation of empirical results, verifying the absolute forecasting accuracy against state-of-the-art baselines is difficult. However, evaluating the methodology raises immediate questions regarding computational efficiency and hardware requirements. 

Despite framing the method as avoiding heavy parametric adaptation, DualWeaver fundamentally requires passing both $S_\alpha$ and $S_\beta$ through the univariate foundation model for all $C$ variables. This natively doubles the number of forward passes ($2C$) compared to standard channel-independent forecasting ($C$). The paper lacks a rigorous discussion justifying this linear scaling factor. A fair empirical comparison must evaluate DualWeaver against channel-independent baselines that are given roughly twice the computational budget (e.g., through ensembling or utilizing a larger backbone). 

Additionally, the methodology presumably requires backpropagating gradients through the frozen foundation model to update the upstream feature-fusion parameters. It is highly uncertain how this framework handles discrete-token, non-differentiable foundation models (such as Chronos). The evaluation appears implicitly restricted to continuous-embedding models, which limits the claimed model-agnostic generality.

- **Parameter-Free Decoding:** The method elegantly solves the pervasive issue of adapter bottlenecking, completely avoiding the allocation of learning capacity to decode latent representations back to the original target space.
- **Architectural Flexibility:** The approach is cleanly modular, allowing the upstream feature-fusion component to be instantiated as MLPs, CNNs, or other temporal architectures without fundamentally altering the training pipeline.
- **Scale-Equivariant Stability:** Assuming instance normalization does not cause pathological collapse, the framework effectively leverages the scale-equivariance property $M(wX) \approx wM(X)$ common in modern TSFMs to ensure a robust fallback to base univariate performance when cross-variable dependencies are unhelpful.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Mathematical Vulnerability (Bias Elimination):** The finite-difference nature of the parameter-free reconstruction step actively strips the base predictions and absolute baseline trends from the foundation model, potentially degrading absolute forecasting accuracy.
- **Optimization Vulnerability (Feature Collapse):** The combination of the $\Omega$ regularizer pushing weights higher and the presence of instance normalization creates a direct mathematical path for the model to bypass the multivariate fusion module entirely, silently collapsing into redundant univariate forecasting.
- **Overclaimed Theory:** The derivations in Equation 11 and 12 rely on trivial algebra rather than robust statistical learning theory.
- **Compute Overhead:** The strictly doubled inference cost ($2C$ forward passes) is significant, and its real-world latency impact must be rigorously justified against the parameter savings.
- **Policy Verification Risk:** The abstract includes an identifiable GitHub link (`https://github.com/li-jinpeng/DualWeaver`). This poses a strict double-blind policy verification risk that the Area Chair must adjudicate against venue guidelines.

Ablation studies comparing Uni-TSFM backbones with and without RevIN are necessary to prove that the fusion features $f(X)$ are not being washed out by large linear scalings.
2. **Linear Backbone Ablation:** Applying DualWeaver to a purely linear forecaster (like DLinear). If the method functions as mathematically written, the non-linearity of the backbone is strictly essential. Demonstrating the expected failure mode on linear models would clarify exactly how the method relies on local Jacobians.
3. **Discrete Token Adaptation:** Clarification or targeted experiments demonstrating how the feature fusion module can be trained when the backbone TSFM utilizes non-differentiable tokenization steps.
4. **Fair Compute Baselines:** Inference throughput (queries/second) and memory footprint comparisons against standard channel-independent baselines, ensuring baselines are scaled to match the $2C$ computational overhead.
