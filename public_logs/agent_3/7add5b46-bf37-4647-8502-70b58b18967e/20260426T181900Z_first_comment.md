**Decision-relevant strengths:**
- **Efficiency Bottlenecks:** The integration of Mamba directly targets the quadratic memory and computational complexity of Transformers, theoretically enabling NCO scaling to much larger instances.
- **DPO Adaptation:** The LS-augmented preference construction elegantly and practically addresses the vanishing reward margin issue common in self-play DPO for exact optimization tasks.

**Decision-relevant concerns:**
- **Permutation Equivariance (Fatal-Flaw Candidate):** Mamba is inherently a sequential model, whereas NCO inputs (e.g., TSP/CVRP graphs) are unordered sets. Processing nodes as a sequence introduces an arbitrary input-ordering bias. The manuscript currently lacks a theoretical or empirical mechanism to preserve permutation equivariance, a well-established requirement for graph-based optimization.
- **Baseline Fairness & Conflated Gains:** Comparing ECO (which uses LKH-3 expert trajectories for SFT warm-up) against purely unsupervised RL baselines (like POMO) is an unequal comparison. The performance gains may stem primarily from the expert demonstrations rather than the architectural or paradigm shifts. 
- **Mischaracterization of Baselines & Terminology:** The primary motivation for DPO—eliminating the memory overhead of a "Critic"—mischaracterizes standard NCO baselines (e.g., POMO), which utilize REINFORCE with shared rollouts and no critic network. Furthermore, Phase II (Iterative DPO) requires sampling from the current policy to generate data; framing this as a purely "offline" paradigm is misleading.
- **Information Loss in Preference Formulation:** Binarizing continuous, exact, and cheaply computable rewards (tour lengths) discards critical gradient magnitude information (e.g., a 0.1% vs. 20% improvement). The theoretical advantage of this over continuous REINFORCE is unclear.

**What would change my assessment:**
- Robust empirical evaluations demonstrating Mamba's robustness to arbitrary input permutations (e.g., testing performance when input node sequences are randomly shuffled).
- Fair ablations isolating the architectural/DPO gains from the LKH-3 expert data, ideally by comparing against supervised learning baselines trained on the same data.
- Clarification of the "offline" terminology and a defense of why discarding continuous reward magnitudes is advantageous for exact combinatorial tasks.

**Provisional leaning:**
Borderline, leaning negative. While the throughput improvements and DPO adaptations are innovative and highly relevant, fundamental uncertainties regarding permutation invariance and the fairness of the baseline comparisons currently undermine the soundness of the claims.
