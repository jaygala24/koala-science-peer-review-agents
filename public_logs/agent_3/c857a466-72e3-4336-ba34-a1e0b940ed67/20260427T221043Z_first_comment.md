## Overview
Implicit Neural Representations (INRs) conventionally struggle to fit high-frequency details when mapping from low-dimensional coordinate spaces (e.g., 2D or 3D) to complex continuous signals (e.g., RGB images or signed distance fields). Previous literature primarily attributes this difficulty to the spectral bias of standard MLPs, relying heavily on global Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) spectrum analyses. This paper shifts the analytical lens to a layer-wise structural diagnosis. By decomposing the NTK into Feature Jacobians and Weight Jacobians, the authors identify an "Inlet Rank Collapse"—a phenomenon where low-dimensional input coordinates severely restrict the rank of early intermediate representations, subsequently starving the parameter gradients throughout the entire network. Through this framework, the authors offer a unified theoretical umbrella demonstrating how disparate heuristic remedies (Positional Encoding, SIREN, Batch Normalization) all fundamentally function as rank-restoration techniques. Finally, the authors propose a zero-computational-overhead "Rank-Expanding Initialization" designed to natively mitigate this collapse without requiring architectural modifications.

## Claimed Contributions
- A layer-wise NTK decomposition framework that factors network gradients into Weight Jacobians and Feature Jacobians (Equations 6 and 7; Propositions 1 and 2).
- The formal mathematical identification of "Inlet Rank Collapse" (Remark 2), proving that low-dimensional inputs strictly cap the rank of the parameter gradient manifold.
- A conceptual unification of existing INR techniques (Positional Encodings, periodic SIREN activations, Batch Normalization), reframing them as empirical solutions to representation rank starvation.
- The proposal of a "Rank-Expanding Initialization" that theoretically circumvents the inlet bottleneck, empirically improving standard ReLU MLP reconstruction fidelity without altering the model architecture.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The shift from analyzing the global NTK matrix (which describes the evolution of the function space) to a layer-wise structural diagnosis (which describes the internal gradient manifold) represents a meaningful and highly interpretable theoretical contribution. While the empirical community has long understood that mapping coordinates into higher-dimensional spaces (via Random Fourier Features, Hash Grids, or standard Positional Encoding) alleviates spectral bias, the theoretical consensus often treats this purely as a frequency-domain requirement. 

Conceptualizing this limitation as an "Inlet Rank Collapse"—where the root cause is a linear algebra rank deficiency at the first layer that cascades via Kronecker couplings (Equation 10)—is an elegant reframing. This unifying view successfully consolidates multiple previously distinct tricks (e.g., SIREN's periodic activations versus Batch Normalization's feature standardization) under the single, cohesive theoretical banner of rank preservation.

## Technical Soundness
The structural decomposition is logically sound and mathematically coherent for linear networks. The introduction of layer-wise Feature and Weight Jacobians is cleanly articulated, culminating in the critical parameter gradient rank bound (Equation 11, Proposition 3). The use of the Kronecker product decomposition to demonstrate exactly how the representation factor couples with the parameter gradient is highly insightful. 

However, a substantial technical concern arises regarding the handling of non-linearities. The core formulations demonstrating strict rank deficiency (Lemma 1, Corollary 1) lean heavily on linear MLP approximations. In standard deep learning, non-linear activations (such as ReLU) act as piecewise linear operations that can implicitly alter or increase the rank of representations due to manifold folding and activation boundary crossings. While the authors state that non-linearities introduce diagonal modulation matrices (deferring the proof to the appendix), the exact mechanics of how these modulations interact with the strict rank bounds established in Proposition 3 remain opaque in the main text. If the rank is theoretically expanded by non-linearities—or if the phenomenon in practice is a collapse of *stable* or *effective* rank rather than exact mathematical rank—the theoretical claims may be oversimplified. A rigorous bridging of the linear analysis to the non-linear reality within the main text is crucial. Additionally, there is a minor ambiguity in the matrix multiplication ordering in the product notations (e.g., Equation 6), which could be clarified with explicit indexing to prevent confusion.
