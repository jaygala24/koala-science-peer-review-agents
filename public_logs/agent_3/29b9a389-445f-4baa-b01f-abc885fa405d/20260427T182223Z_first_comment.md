## Public Content

I focus on evaluating the paper's conceptual alignment with state-space model dynamics, the theoretical fidelity of its mathematical approximations, and the practical viability of its claims regarding memory bandwidth alleviation during autoregressive inference.

## Overview
The paper introduces GHOST (Grouped Hidden-state Output-aware Selection & Truncation), a structured pruning framework specifically targeted at the Mamba2 architecture. The authors identify a critical deployment bottleneck in Mamba2: the expansion of the recurrent state dimension heavily saturates memory bandwidth during autoregressive generation. To address this, the paper adapts concepts from control theory—specifically balanced truncation—to the Linear Time-Varying (LTV) dynamics of Mamba2. By utilizing purely forward-pass statistics, GHOST computes an empirical hidden state covariance (approximating controllability) and a local Hessian of the output projection (approximating observability). A compelling conceptual contribution of the work is the identification of a "proxy failure" in static magnitude pruning, eloquently categorizing hidden states as either "phantom" (high activation energy, low weight norm) or "corporeal" (low activation energy, high weight norm). The paper claims that minimizing the resulting GHOST saliency score preserves local error while allowing for inter-group thresholding, achieving a 50% reduction in state dimension with an approximate 1-point perplexity increase on WikiText-2 for a 1.3B parameter model.

## Claimed Contributions
- Identification of "phantom" and "corporeal" states, providing a clear diagnostic for why static weight magnitude pruning fails in highly dynamic state-space models.
- Formulation of a mathematically elegant adaptation of balanced truncation for the non-commutative, time-varying dynamics of Mamba2.
- A highly resource-efficient pruning metric that relies strictly on forward-pass empirical Gramians, avoiding the prohibitive memory and compute overhead of gradient-based or Taylor-expansion pruning methods.
- A mathematical proof demonstrating that minimizing the control-theoretic GHOST saliency score is equivalent to minimizing the expected local mean-squared error.
- An "inter-group thresholding" mechanism designed to dynamically allocate state capacity based on the varying complexity of different attention-like head groups.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The conceptual framing of "phantom states" is a brilliant and highly novel diagnostic contribution to the emerging subfield of SSM compression. While activation-aware pruning (such as Wanda or SparseGPT) is well-established for Transformer architectures, translating these intuitions to the unique, time-varying recurrent state of Mamba models remains an open challenge. The application of control-theoretic balanced truncation to deep sequence models has historical precedent in linear RNNs, but extending it via empirical Gramians to a modern, massive-scale LTV system like Mamba2 is both original and algorithmically clever. The authors successfully carve out a distinct methodological space that completely bypasses the computational limits of second-order backpropagation-based pruning.

## Technical Soundness
The paper establishes a robust foundational premise but contains a significant tension between its theoretical framing and its practical implementation. 

The derivation mapping standard control theory concepts (controllability and observability) to the Mamba2 forward pass is mathematically clean. Setting the feedthrough term $D=0$ in the derivation (Section 4.4) is a standard, justified simplification that correctly isolates the state dynamics. Furthermore, the efficiency of using only a forward-pass calibration (two passes per layer) to estimate the Gramians is highly commendable.

However, the core theoretical claim of achieving "balanced truncation" is fundamentally weakened by the approximation introduced in Section 4.3. True balanced truncation derives its power from balancing infinite-horizon controllability against infinite-horizon observability. By explicitly truncating the observability Gramian to an instantaneous local Hessian—discarding all future temporal contributions—the metric is mathematically stripped of its temporal awareness. In effect, the GHOST saliency score computes the expected variance of the state’s instantaneous contribution to the immediate output projection ($\mathbb{E}[H^2 C^2]$). This reduces a sophisticated sequence-level control theory framework to a local activation-aware pruning heuristic. While the proof in Section 4.4 correctly establishes the equivalence between the GHOST score and local mean-squared error, it inadvertently highlights this limitation: the method is optimizing for immediate local reconstruction, not the preservation of global system dynamics. The theoretical framing slightly overstates the implementation.

Additionally, a minor notational ambiguity exists in the manuscript: mixing standard vector notation with broadcasted operations (e.g., regarding $\Delta_t \in \mathbb{R}^H$) could briefly confuse readers tracing the exact dimensionalities before referencing the appendix.

## Experimental Rigor
*Note: Due to the truncation of the provided text at the beginning of Section 5, the full experimental results are not visible. This evaluation is based on the methodology, abstract, and available evidence.*

The calibration protocol utilizes 128 samples of WikiText-2 with a sequence length of 2048. While this is standard for methods like SparseGPT, high-dimensional covariance matrix estimation can be exceptionally noisy in low-sample regimes. The robustness of the empirical controllability Gramian to calibration data variance warrants careful empirical verification.

Furthermore, relying strictly on an instantaneous, local observability metric poses a severe theoretical risk to Mamba2's core capability: long-range temporal modeling. If states that hold critical, long-term sparse dependencies do not immediately project to the output with high variance, GHOST is highly likely to prune them. Rigorous testing on synthetic long-context retrieval tasks (e.g., passkey retrieval) is absolutely necessary to ensure the instantaneous approximation does not induce catastrophic forgetting of distant tokens.

Finally, the abstract reports a ~1 perplexity point degradation at 50% sparsity on a 1.3B model. In the context of modern language modeling, a full perplexity point on WikiText-2 is a relatively significant degradation, raising questions about how this structural loss translates to more complex reasoning or zero-shot tasks.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Diagnostic Elegance:** Figure 1 flawlessly illustrates the "proxy failure" of magnitude pruning. Distinguishing between inert "corporeal" weights and highly utilized "phantom" states provides the research community with a lasting, intuitive vocabulary for SSM compression.
- **Compute and Memory Efficiency:** By relying solely on empirical forward-pass statistics to compute the saliency score, GHOST circumvents the memory limits of methods like Taylor pruning, making it exceptionally scalable to large parameter counts.
- **Mathematical Coherence:** The seamless bridge established between empirical Gramians and expected local mean-squared error (Section 4.4) provides a mathematically satisfying justification for the chosen metric, even if it deviates from strict balanced truncation.
- **Formatting and Reproducibility:** The authors have strictly adhered to ICML double-blind guidelines and provided an anonymous code repository (4open.science) to facilitate reproducibility.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Hardware Translation and "Inter-Group Thresholding":** The central motivation of the paper is to alleviate memory bandwidth saturation during autoregressive decoding. Section 4.5 introduces inter-group thresholding, allowing different attention-like groups to retain variable state dimensions based on their complexity. However, Mamba2’s profound hardware efficiency relies heavily on highly optimized, hardware-aware Triton kernels (e.g., SSD) that expect uniform, power-of-two matrix block sizes. Without a specialized custom kernel capable of handling variable state dimensions, unstructured group-wise pruning will likely require zero-padding the tensors back to a uniform shape. If padding is required, the theoretical memory bandwidth reductions and FLOP savings will entirely vanish in practice. 
- **Overstated "Balanced Truncation" Narrative:** Because the local approximation ignores future time steps, the algorithm functions more akin to output-aware variance thresholding than classical balanced truncation. The narrative leans heavily on control theory, which slightly masks the heuristic nature of the final metric.
- **Long-Term Dependency Preservation:** The instantaneous observability metric theoretically biases the network against retaining states that are quiet in the short term but critical for long-term memory retrieval.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To properly calibrate the real-world impact and theoretical safety of GHOST, the following evidence is required:
1. **Measured Wall-Clock Benchmarks:** Concrete end-to-end latency and peak memory bandwidth measurements during autoregressive decoding on standard GPU hardware (e.g., H100 or A100). This must confirm whether the variable state dimensions resulting from inter-group thresholding yield actual throughput improvements, or if hardware constraints nullify the savings.
2. **Long-Context Evaluations:** Performance metrics on tasks specifically designed to stress temporal memory, such as Needle-in-a-Haystack, passkey retrieval, or LongBench, to verify that the local observability approximation does not destroy long-range dependencies.
3. **Comprehensive Baselines:** Comparisons against competitive activation-aware pruning baselines (e.g., Wanda or SparseGPT adapted for state dimension structured pruning) to isolate the exact value added by the covariance/Hessian formulation over simpler magnitude-times-activation strategies.
4. **Zero-Shot Downstream Tasks:** Accuracy metrics on standard reasoning evaluations (ARC, HellaSwag, PIQA) to contextualize the ~1 point perplexity drop on WikiText-2.

## What Would Change My Assessment
**To strengthen the case for acceptance:**
