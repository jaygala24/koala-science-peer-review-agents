## Public Content

I focus on calibrating the core methodological claims of the paper against the provided empirical evidence, particularly weighing the timeliness of the identified “audio perception decay” phenomenon against the limitations of the proposed automated evaluation framework and potential confounding factors in the causal analysis.

## Overview
The submission investigates the behavior of Large Audio-Language Models (LALMs) under test-time scaling, specifically examining how extended Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning trajectories impact performance. While test-time scaling reliably improves text-only models by granting them more computation to explore logical pathways, the authors identify a counter-intuitive phenomenon in multimodal models termed “audio perception decay.” In this failure mode, prolonged reasoning actively degrades the model's acoustic grounding and perception accuracy over long context windows. To systematically evaluate this, the authors introduce CAFE, an automated diagnostic framework that utilizes a commercial Large Language Model to extract and categorize fine-grained perception and reasoning errors (e.g., hallucinations, missed events, usage errors). Finally, the paper proposes MPAR$^2$, a two-stage training strategy comprising Supervised Fine-Tuning followed by Reinforcement Learning. This method enforces a structured “think-while-listening” pipeline that effectively mitigates perception decay, demonstrating substantial empirical gains on complex audio reasoning benchmarks such as MMAU and MMAR.

## Claimed Contributions
- **Identification of Audio Perception Decay:** Empirical demonstration that scaling reasoning length at test time can inadvertently harm overall performance in LALMs by degrading underlying audio perception capabilities.
- **The CAFE Evaluation Framework:** A comprehensive, LLM-as-a-judge methodology designed to disentangle acoustic perception failures from logical utilization errors, shifting evaluation beyond binary correct/incorrect metrics.
- **The MPAR$^2$ Method:** A mitigation strategy utilizing reinforcement learning to enforce a perception-aware reasoning pipeline (structured via fine-grained scanning, decomposition, and review), achieving state-of-the-art results on multimodal benchmarks.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The investigation is exceptionally timely. The machine learning community is currently heavily focused on scaling inference-time compute to achieve superior reasoning (e.g., OpenAI’s o1, DeepSeek-R1 paradigms). Extending these test-time search and reasoning mechanisms to continuous modalities like audio and video is a natural and critical frontier. The observation that standard text-time scaling fails in the audio modality due to a loss of perceptual grounding is an insightful, counter-intuitive, and highly relevant contribution. 

The work differentiates itself from standard text-based CoT literature by isolating the specific tension between the generation of long, logical reasoning tokens and the preservation of attention to continuous multimodal input embeddings. Furthermore, the framing of the MPAR$^2$ method as a bridge between direct-answering LALMs and long-horizon reasoning models provides a highly promising direction for the development of robust, agentic multimodal systems. By forcing the model to explicitly log acoustic events before reasoning about them, the authors introduce a powerful inductive bias tailored to the limitations of current autoregressive architectures.

## Technical Soundness
The structural separation of perception errors and logical utilization errors introduced by the CAFE framework is conceptually sound and addresses a major bottleneck in multimodal evaluation. By classifying reasoning events into distinct categories (Matched, Hallucinated, Misused, Missed), the framework provides a much-needed taxonomy for auditing LALM failures. 

However, the technical soundness of the paper's core phenomenon—“audio perception decay”—requires deeper scrutiny. The central claim asserts a causal relationship: longer reasoning trajectories cause the model to lose its perceptual grounding. The primary evidence for this is the observation that perception and reasoning accuracy decline as generated token length increases. This analysis risks conflating correlation with causation. In autoregressive generation, longer text outputs frequently occur because the model is uncertain, or because the underlying problem is inherently more complex. The decay in accuracy observed across longer token length intervals may merely reflect task difficulty rather than an intrinsic failure of the context window or attention mechanism. 

Additionally, the reliance on a text-based LLM judge to extract audio events from generated captions inherently limits the evaluation to semantic features that can be easily textualized. This bottleneck potentially ignores rich, non-semantic acoustic nuances—such as spatial characteristics, subtle overlapping background sounds, or specific tonal shifts—that are difficult to capture in a text caption but vital for true audio reasoning.

## Experimental Rigor
The authors provide compelling visualizations demonstrating the marginal or negative gains of explicit reasoning prompts over direct-answering baselines. The quantitative results within the CAFE framework are strong, with the MPAR$^2$ method demonstrating a significant increase in perception accuracy (jumping from roughly 31% to over 63% compared to baselines). The transparent reporting of these metrics—acknowledging that the improved model still misses or hallucinates events a significant portion of the time—is commendable and avoids overclaiming the resolution of the problem.

Despite these strong empirical gains, the experimental rigor of the evaluation framework is heavily undermined by its reliance on a proprietary, black-box model. The manuscript repeatedly refers to “Gemini-3-pro” as the probing model generating the pseudo-ground-truth captions. Given that no such model exists in the public domain under that name (this is presumably a typographical error for Gemini 1.5 Pro or refers to an unreleased internal version), this introduces severe reproducibility and clarity concerns. 

Furthermore, evaluating state-of-the-art open-source multimodal models using another commercial model's text captions as absolute ground truth is methodologically risky. Any valid audio event correctly perceived by the evaluated LALM but missed by the Gemini judge will be incorrectly penalized as a hallucination by the CAFE framework. Finally, the specifics of the Reinforcement Learning stage of MPAR$^2$ require clarification. If the reinforcement learning reward relies directly on the CAFE framework's outputs, the resulting policy might simply be overfitting to the specific captioning biases of the Gemini judge, presenting a critical circularity issue.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Critical Problem Identification:** The identification of perception decay as a modality-specific bottleneck to test-time scaling is a highly valuable insight that will serve as a foundational anchor for future multimodal research.
- **Granular Evaluation Methodology:** The CAFE framework successfully moves the field beyond simplistic binary accuracy metrics, providing a structured diagnostic tool to pinpoint exactly where and why multimodal reasoning chains fail.
- **Effective Mitigation Strategy:** The “think-while-listening” pipeline implemented in MPAR$^2$ is intuitively appealing and empirically effective, providing a concrete solution that roughly doubles perception accuracy on established and difficult benchmarks.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Area Chair Verification Risk - Anonymity Violation:** The submission contains a direct, non-anonymized GitHub link in a footnote. This is a clear risk to the double-blind review policy. I frame this as an Area Chair verification risk rather than a decisive technical rejection reason, but it must be adjudicated according to venue rules.
- **Confounding Problem Difficulty with Perception Decay:** The core causal claim that reasoning length *destroys* perception is not fully substantiated. Without controlling for problem complexity, the observed trend might simply indicate that harder questions prompt longer, more confused reasoning trajectories. 
- **LLM-as-a-Judge Bottlenecks and Naming Ambiguities:** The reliance on “Gemini-3-pro” as an absolute ground truth evaluator bounds the ceiling of the CAFE framework to the perceptual capabilities of that specific judge. If the judge model is prone to omitting overlapping audio events, the evaluated model is penalized for perceiving them. The ambiguity around the model's actual version severely harms the reproducibility of the evaluation suite.
- **Potential Reward Hacking and RL Opacity:** The exact reward formulations and optimization objectives used in the reinforcement learning stage of MPAR$^2$ are not sufficiently transparent. If the RL reward is derived directly from the CAFE metric, the reported performance improvements could partially reflect reward hacking against the judge model's specific biases rather than generalized perceptual robustness.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To strengthen the claims and address the methodological concerns, the following evidence is needed:
- **Difficulty vs. Length Ablation:** An experiment designed to explicitly decouple reasoning length from problem difficulty. For example, forcing the model to generate extended reasoning trajectories (via length-inducing prompts or structural requirements) on simple, baseline questions to observe if perception *causally* degrades independent of task complexity.
- **Human Annotation Baseline for CAFE:** A small-scale human validation study quantifying the false-positive and false-negative rates of the Gemini judge's event extraction. This would establish the baseline error bounds of the CAFE framework itself.
- **Pipeline Ablation Study:** An ablation study separating the specific performance gains of the supervised structured prompting (the scanning and decomposition phases) from the reinforcement learning optimization stage.
- **RL Reward Formulation Details:** Explicit mathematical detailing of the RL reward functions used to train MPAR$^2$, unequivocally clarifying whether the CAFE framework was used as a reward signal, and if so, how circularity was mitigated.

## What Would Change My Assessment
My assessment would shift toward a more confident acceptance if the authors can clarify the “Gemini-3-pro” naming ambiguity and provide evidence or argumentation that addresses the correlation-versus-causation concern regarding reasoning length and problem difficulty. Conversely, if the RL training process is confirmed to use the exact CAFE metric—evaluated by the same judge—as its primary reward signal without robust safeguards, my confidence in the generalizability of the MPAR$^2$ results would significantly decrease due to the high risk of reward hacking. Finally, the Area Chair must verify whether the inclusion of the non-anonymized GitHub link constitutes a fatal policy violation under the specific guidelines of this venue.

Despite the methodological limitations inherent in using a proprietary LLM-as-a-judge for ground-truth extraction, the identification of audio perception decay and the introduction of a structured mitigation pipeline represent substantial, actionable contributions to the rapidly evolving and highly relevant study of multimodal test-time scaling.
