I focus on the conceptual formulation, theoretical motivation, and structural completeness of the proposed framework, Information-Aware Credit Assignment (ICA). 

## Overview
This paper targets two fundamental bottlenecks in training long-horizon, information-seeking large language model (LLM) agents: the severe observation noise introduced by heuristic HTML-to-text parsing, and the extreme difficulty of credit assignment in environments with sparse, terminal-only rewards. To resolve the first issue, the authors propose transitioning the agent's observation space to a visual-native format, utilizing rendered webpage snapshots to preserve crucial spatial, structural, and visual semantics. To resolve the second, the authors introduce Information-Aware Credit Assignment (ICA), a post-hoc mechanism designed to reverse-engineer the contribution of specific "atomic evidence units" acquired during navigation. By mapping these evidence units to successful task completion, ICA theoretically converts sparse terminal signals into dense, turn-level rewards without the heavy computational burden of online, step-by-step LLM-as-a-judge supervision. The framework is integrated into a Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) pipeline. 

Unfortunately, the manuscript provided for review is critically truncated. The text abruptly ends in Section 4.1.2, precisely halfway through the mathematical formulation of the ICA mechanism. Consequently, the entire experimental setup, all baseline comparisons, ablation studies, and technical implementation details are missing. My assessment extensively evaluates the high conceptual promise of the available theoretical text, while explicitly documenting the severe evidentiary void that precludes acceptance in its current state.

## Claimed Contributions
- **Diagnosis of Representation Bottlenecks**: Identifying textual parsing heuristics (e.g., stripping HTML tags, flattening DOM trees) and sparse terminal rewards as fundamental limitations in current web-agent reinforcement learning.
- **Visual-Native Observation Space**: Advocating for the use of rendered high-resolution webpage snapshots as the primary environmental state representation, thereby bypassing fragile text-based parsing pipelines and preserving semantic layout cues.
- **Information-Aware Credit Assignment (ICA)**: Proposing a novel, post-hoc attribution mechanism that estimates the granular contribution of acquired "atomic evidence units" to final task success, thereby providing dense learning signals for intermediate search actions.
- **GRPO Integration**: Embedding visual grounding and evidence-level credit assignment into a GRPO-based training pipeline tailored for open-ended web environments.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The conceptual framing of this paper represents a strong, highly relevant contribution to the frontier of autonomous web navigation. Framing web navigation strictly as a text-based Partially Observable Markov Decision Process (POMDP) has historically introduced severe aliasing; crucial contextual cues like element proximity, font hierarchies, and spatial layout are destroyed when a webpage is flattened into a DOM tree or Markdown. Recent literature (e.g., WebVoyager, Multimodal WebArena) has begun exploring visual web navigation, but effectively integrating visual state spaces into a long-horizon reinforcement learning pipeline remains a largely unsolved challenge. 

The paper's secondary conceptual leap—decoupling the raw act of information acquisition from the estimation of its value via a post-hoc, evidence-centric mechanism—is highly novel. Standard credit assignment in long-horizon RL (especially within GRPO) often suffers from the "credit assignment problem" where every action in a successful 20-step trajectory receives a positive advantage, even if 15 of those steps were useless wandering. By identifying discrete "atomic evidence units" (the specific snapshots acquired) and probabilistically mapping their presence to trajectory success across a batch of rollouts, ICA theoretically provides a highly elegant, compute-efficient alternative to online dense-reward generators (such as step-wise LLM evaluation or IGPO). Conceptually, this is a sophisticated and highly original approach to multi-turn multimodal RL.

## Technical Soundness
The theoretical framing provided in Sections 1 through 4.1.1 is exceptionally well-motivated and structurally sound. The diagnosis of HTML parsing noise is accurate, and the theoretical integration of ICA into the batch-sampling structure of GRPO makes logical sense. Utilizing the variance in outcomes across multiple rollouts to isolate the marginal utility of specific acquired evidence is an optimal use of GRPO's group-based architecture.

However, the technical soundness of the ICA mechanism itself cannot be fully verified due to the manuscript's truncation. The text introduces the concept of Turn-Level Credit Assignment in Section 4.1.2, stating: *"The core idea is to assign credit based on how strongly acquired external observations are associated with successful task completion..."* It then proceeds to Equation 7, intended to formalize the "Atomic-Evidence Counterfactual Contribution," but cuts off mid-sentence with *"To address the challen"*. 

Based on the available formulation leading up to Equation 7, several critical technical uncertainties emerge:
1. **Context Window Scalability**: Accumulating multiple high-resolution webpage snapshots over long-horizon rollouts (often 10–30 steps in web navigation) necessitates massive context windows. Modern Vision-Language Models degrade in reasoning quality and face severe quadratic computational scaling as image tokens increase. The text does not explain how context overflow or image token limits are managed. Are previous snapshots discarded? Are they downsampled or compressed into a memory vector store? 
2. **Confounding Variables in Credit Assignment**: If the ICA formulation merely correlates the presence of a visual snapshot with outcome success, it risks reinforcing spurious correlations. For instance, an agent might frequently visit the homepage of a domain before eventually succeeding, leading ICA to heavily weight the homepage snapshot even if it provided no actionable evidence. How does the complete counterfactual formulation dynamically isolate the causal impact of specific reasoning dependencies from mere statistical correlation?

## Experimental Rigor
There is currently zero experimental rigor to evaluate. The manuscript ends before any empirical setup, benchmark description, baseline comparison, or ablation study is introduced. While the abstract and introduction make robust claims about the approach *"consistently outperforming text-based baselines"* on diverse benchmarks, there is absolutely no evidence provided in the artifact to substantiate this. 

In long-horizon agent literature, performance gains can stem from a variety of orthogonal factors: better base models, longer context windows, brute-force sampling, or specific reward shaping. Without a rigorous experimental section, it is functionally impossible to determine if the proposed ICA mechanism is actually responsible for the claimed performance improvements. 

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Exceptional Problem Formulation**: The paper perfectly identifies two of the most pressing bottlenecks in visually grounded agents today: the brittleness of heuristic text representations and the credit-assignment ambiguity inherent in sparse-reward long-horizon trajectories.
- **Elegant Architectural Design**: Leveraging GRPO’s inherent batch structure to generate dense rewards post-hoc is a brilliant strategy. It avoids the prohibitive computational cost of querying an LLM-as-a-judge at every intermediate step during training.
- **Strong Alignment with Foundation Model Trends**: Multimodal LLMs are increasingly capable of native visual processing. Aligning the RL environment to directly utilize these capabilities—rather than forcing a lossy text bottleneck—is the correct long-term architectural direction for web agents.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Fatal Incompleteness**: The manuscript is severely truncated, cutting off in the middle of Equation 7 in Section 4.1.2. This renders the primary technical contribution and all empirical claims entirely unverifiable.
- **Unverified Computational Overhead**: Processing high-dimensional visual snapshots within an RL pipeline—especially generating multiple rollout trajectories simultaneously for GRPO—is exceptionally demanding on VRAM and compute. The paper lacks any discussion on the memory footprint, context token usage, or training wall-clock time required for visual-native search compared to text-parsing baselines.
- **Potential for Causal Confounding**: Without seeing the completion of the counterfactual contribution math, there is significant concern that the credit assignment mechanism might reward the acquisition of correlated but non-causal visual evidence, thereby reinforcing suboptimal looping or wandering behaviors.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To render this paper actionable and verifiable for an ICML-style venue, the following must be provided in a complete manuscript:
1. **The Complete Mathematical Formulation**: Specifically, the completion of Equation 7 and the full explanation of how the Atomic-Evidence Counterfactual Contribution is computed, bounded, and back-propagated to the specific reasoning actions that acquired it.
2. **Rigorous Ablation Studies**: It is absolutely crucial to disentangle the performance gains yielded by the visual-native representation from the gains provided by the ICA reward mechanism. An acceptable experimental section must evaluate:
   - *Text Parsing + Sparse Reward* (Standard Baseline)
   - *Visual Snapshots + Sparse Reward* (Isolates the benefit of visual representation)
   - *Text Parsing + ICA* (Isolates the benefit of the credit assignment method)

3. **Comprehensive Benchmark Results**: Quantitative evaluations on standard information-seeking benchmarks (e.g., WebArena, Mind2Web, WebVoyager) demonstrating the precise magnitude of improvement over current state-of-the-art text-based baselines (e.g., DeepMMSearch-R1, WebDancer).
4. **Computational Cost Profiling**: A transparent breakdown of the memory usage, token consumption, and compute costs associated with encoding multiple visual snapshots during GRPO rollouts.
