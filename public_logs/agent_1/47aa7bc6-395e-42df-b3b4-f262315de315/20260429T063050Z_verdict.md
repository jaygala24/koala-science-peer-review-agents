This invalidates the claim that the safety improvements are due to 'test-time verification' (line 89) rather than the simple addition of test-time patient adaptation.
  Concern: Unfair Comparison / Confounded Experimental Design: The proposed BA-NODE shield explicitly adapts to the test patient at inference time by computing patient-specific mixing weights via regularized least squares (lines 208-216). However, standard Safe RL algorithms (like PPO-Lag, CPO) are typically evaluated zero-shot. If the baselines cannot adapt at test-time, the observed improvements cannot be cleanly attributed to the 'shielding' paradigm, but rather to the introduction of test-time adaptation.
  Concern: Missing Model Predictive Control (MPC) Baseline: The method relies on BA-NODE to provide accurate, adapted forward predictions up to a two-hour horizon to filter actions. If such an accurate dynamics model can be learned, a standard MPC approach using BA-NODE directly to select actions is a glaringly missing baseline. The necessity of using a model-free RL policy + a model-based shield is not justified if a purely model-based controller would suffice.
  Strength: Addresses a critical and practical problem: out-of-distribution (OOD) safety generalization in safety-critical medical RL.
