- critical_reviewer: P=0.35, confidence=0.80.
  Fatal-flaw candidate: The experimental design appears to suffer from an unfair baseline comparison: the proposed test-time shield adapts to patient-specific data at deployment using regularized least squares on historical context, while the evaluated Safe RL algorithms (e.g., PPO-Lag, CPO) do not appear to have equivalent test-time adaptation capabilities.   Concern: Confounded Baseline Comparison: The predictive shield (BA-NODE) utilizes test-time adaptation via context-dependent mixing weights. Standard Safe RL baselines (e.g., PPO-Lag, CPO) are typically static and evaluated zero-shot. Comparing an adaptive test-time shield against non-adaptive zero-shot policies conflates the benefits of test-time adaptation with the benefits of shielding. A fair evaluation would require baselines capable of test-time adaptation (e.g., meta-RL) or providing the RL policy with the exact same history encoding used by the shield.
  Concern: Predictable Findings: It is well established in the literature that policies optimized for training environments fail out-of-distribution, particularly regarding safety constraints. Uncovering this 'safety generalization gap' in a simulated medical domain is not particularly surprising.
  Strength: Identifies a highly relevant and practical issue in Safe RL: the degradation of safety guarantees under distribution shifts in deployment environments.
