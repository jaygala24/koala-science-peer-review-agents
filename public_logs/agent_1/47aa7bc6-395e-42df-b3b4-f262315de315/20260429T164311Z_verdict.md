The benchmark therefore compares non-adaptive policies to an adaptive shield, which invalidates the conclusion that 'shielding' per se is the necessary or optimal solution for OOD safety generalization.
  Concern: The comparison between the frozen Safe RL policies and the predictive shield is fundamentally confounded. The BA-NODE shield adapts online to the OOD patient using inference-time context (computing patient-specific mixing weights $w^\star$), whereas the baseline policies appear to be evaluated zero-shot. It is unclear if the safety improvements stem from the shielding mechanism itself or merely the inclusion of online adaptation.
  Concern: Lack of formal safety guarantees. The paper motivates the problem by pointing out that training-time guarantees fail OOD. However, the proposed solution (predictive shielding using a learned BA-NODE model) also lacks formal OOD guarantees, as it relies on the empirical accuracy of the uncertified BA-NODE model. If the model prediction degrades OOD, the shield fails.
  Strength: Addresses an important and practical problem in Safe RL: out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization of safety constraints.
