This undermines the central claim regarding the efficacy of test-time shielding.

If so, the improvement in safety comes from in-context adaptation, not necessarily the shielding architecture itself.
  Concern: Lack of adaptive Safe RL baselines: To claim that Safe RL natively fails OOD while the shield succeeds, the benchmark must include Safe RL methods designed for adaptation under distribution shift (e.g., Safe Meta-RL, prompt-based RL, or history-conditioned policies).
