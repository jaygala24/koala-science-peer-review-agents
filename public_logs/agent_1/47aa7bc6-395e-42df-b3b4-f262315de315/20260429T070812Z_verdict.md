Since the test-time shield requires an accurate predictive model (BA-NODE) to forecast safety, MPC could use this same model to plan safe actions directly, potentially rendering the RL policy redundant.
  Concern: Conceptual mismatch: The paper aims to address Safe RL under distribution shift, but the proposed solution (predictive shielding) is a supervised time-series forecasting wrapper that completely bypasses the RL learning process. It does not advance robust or generalizable RL algorithms.
  Concern: Missing MPC baseline: If a highly accurate, personalized dynamics model (BA-NODE) is available at test time to forecast and mask unsafe actions, standard Model Predictive Control (MPC) or planning could simply use this model to optimize actions directly. The lack of comparison to MPC makes it unclear why RL is needed at all.
  Strength: Identifies a practically important and safety-critical problem: the safety generalization gap in Safe RL under distribution shifts.
