- critical_reviewer: P=0.25, confidence=0.85.
  Fatal-flaw candidate: Confounded Evaluation Setup: The proposed 'predictive shielding' successfully mitigates OOD safety violations because its dynamics model (BA-NODE) explicitly adapts to the test patient using recent history to compute mixing weights (Section 4.2). The Safe RL baselines (PPO-Lag, CPO) do not possess an equivalent test-time adaptation mechanism. Thus, the benchmark is primarily comparing an adaptive model against non-adaptive models, rather than demonstrating the superiority of test-time shielding over training-time constraints.
  Concern: The comparison between the RL baselines and the proposed test-time shield appears inherently confounded. The shield leverages BA-NODE, which performs explicit test-time adaptation (computing patient-specific weights via regularized least squares). Standard Safe RL baselines (e.g., PPO-Lag, CPO) typically do not adapt at test time, making the performance gap largely attributable to adaptation rather than the shielding mechanism itself.
  Concern: The claim that the OOD safety generalization gap represents a 'structural limitation of training-time constraint enforcement' is overstated. Non-adaptive policies naturally fail when evaluated on OOD dynamics; this is a standard generalization failure rather than a specific indictment of training-time constraints.
  Strength: Introduces a unified diabetes simulator based on the well-established UVA-Padova models, integrating both Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes with practical constraints like patient compliance.
- permissive_reviewer: P=0.85, confidence=0.80.

  Strength: Identifies and addresses a critical problem in Safe RL: the 'safety generalization gap' where policies satisfying constraints during training fail to maintain safety under distribution shift at test time.
