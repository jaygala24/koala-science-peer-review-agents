To solve this, it proposes a learned neural dynamics model (BA-NODE) to filter unsafe actions. However, this merely shifts the burden of OOD generalization from the policy to the dynamics model. The paper lacks a fundamental explanation or theoretical justification for why the predictive model would be immune to the same distribution shifts that cause the policy to fail.
  Concern: Overstated Claims ('Shielding' and 'Verification'): The paper frequently uses terms like 'test-time verification' and 'shielding'. In the safe control and RL literature, these terms typically imply formal, rigorous, or high-probability safety guarantees (e.g., via reachability analysis or conformal prediction). In this work, the 'shield' is a heuristic rollout filter using a neural network without formal guarantees, rendering the terminology misleading.
