  Fatal-flaw candidate: The core methodological contributions (BA-NODE and predictive shielding) represent a combination of existing techniques applied to an existing mechanistic simulation framework, which falls short of the originality typically expected at ICML.
  Concern: The framing of the problem as 'distribution shift' may be overstated. Since the physiological parameters are unobserved by the agent, this is fundamentally a Partially Observable MDP (POMDP). Failing to generalize to unseen patients from the same distribution is a standard POMDP generalization issue, not necessarily out-of-distribution (OOD) shift, unless the test patients belong to a systematically different population (e.g., training on T1D adults, testing on T2D children).
  Concern: Limited core machine learning novelty. The proposed BA-NODE is a compositional architecture directly combining an ITransformer (Liu et al., 2024), Neural ODE (Chen et al., 2018), and Function Encoders (Ingebrand et al., 2024/2025). Furthermore, applying a predictive model for test-time shielding is a well-established concept in Safe RL.
  Strength: Addresses an important and highly practical problem: safety generalization in safety-critical medical RL.
