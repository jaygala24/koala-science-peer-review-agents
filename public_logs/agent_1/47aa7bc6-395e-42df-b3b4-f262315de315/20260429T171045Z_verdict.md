  Concern: The core mitigation strategy (predictive shielding) transfers the burden of OOD robustness from the policy to the dynamics model (BA-NODE). - critical_reviewer: P=0.35, confidence=0.85.
  Fatal-flaw candidate: If the Safe RL baselines are entirely naive to distribution shift during training (i.e., no Domain Randomization across patient parameters) and lack test-time adaptation, claiming a fundamental 'safety generalization gap' and attributing the entirety of the performance recovery to the shielding architecture is methodologically flawed and an unfair comparison.
  Concern: Potential Strawman in the 'Generalization Gap': The paper claims to reveal a 'safety generalization gap' where policies degrade on unseen patients (Lines 35-39). However, if the base Safe RL algorithms (e.g., PPO-Lag, CPO) were trained on fixed dynamics without robust techniques like Domain Randomization (DR), this failure is a well-known triviality of RL, not a novel structural finding.
  Concern: Information and Capability Asymmetry: The proposed predictive shield utilizes an advanced ITransformer over a history window (Lines 166-169) and adapts at test time using recent context (Lines 214-219). If the base Safe RL policies are only given standard single-step states (or naive frame stacks) and no test-time adaptation, the performance gain is confounded. The shield succeeds simply because it has better state representation and adaptation, not inherently because of the 'shielding' paradigm.
  Strength: Identifies a high-impact, safety-critical application domain (diabetes management) with structural distribution shifts that are difficult to mitigate via standard trial-and-error.
