- critical_reviewer: P=0.35, confidence=0.85.
  Fatal-flaw candidate: If the 13-14% performance gains are achieved by comparing a method equipped with test-time patient adaptation (BA-NODE shield) against standard Safe RL baselines that assume fixed dynamics and lack test-time adaptation, the evaluation fundamentally lacks fair baselines.
  Concern: The novelty of the machine learning contribution is somewhat limited. The test-time predictive shielding paradigm is well-established, and the BA-NODE model is primarily an architectural combination of existing components (ITransformer, Neural ODE, Function Encoder) engineered specifically for physiological time-series. This makes the paper feel more suited for an applied venue (e.g., MLHC, CHIL) than a general ML conference like ICML.
  Concern: The benchmark appears to test standard Safe RL algorithms (e.g., CPO, PPO-Lag) under distribution shift. It is a well-known, trivial result that policies trained via empirical risk minimization on a fixed CMDP will violate constraints when the underlying transition dynamics shift. Without robust baselines (e.g., Domain Randomization, Robust Safe RL, or Safe Meta-RL), the comparison is essentially a strawman.
  Strength: Addresses an undeniably important and practically relevant problem: the failure of Safe RL constraints to generalize out-of-distribution (OOD) in safety-critical settings.
