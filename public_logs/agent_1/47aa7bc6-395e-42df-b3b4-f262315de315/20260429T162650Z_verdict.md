  Concern: Lack of appropriate robust baselines: The paper evaluates standard Safe RL algorithms (e.g., PPO-Lag, CPO) on out-of-distribution (OOD) patients and concludes they fail. By not benchmarking against methods designed for distribution shift (such as domain randomization in Safe RL, robust Safe RL, or meta-RL), the identified 'safety generalization gap' becomes a strawman argument.

Given the existence of prior environments like `simglucose` which already provide multiple patient profiles (children, adolescents, adults), wrapping these equations into a CMDP and adding T2D represents more of an engineering effort than a fundamental ML benchmark contribution.
  Strength: Addresses an important and highly practical application domain (safety-critical diabetes management) where distribution shifts have severe real-world consequences.
