- critical_reviewer: P=0.35, confidence=0.80.
  Fatal-flaw candidate: The proposed test-time predictive shielding relies on a predictor (BA-NODE) that adapts to the test patient using inference-time context. Evaluating an adaptive shield paired with a frozen policy against purely frozen zero-shot policies does not truly 'solve' or 'bridge' the zero-shot safety generalization gap; it circumvents it by allowing test-time adaptation in the auxiliary predictive module. This fundamentally confounds the benchmark's claims about zero-shot generalization.
  Concern: Problem Shifting & Unfair Baseline Comparison: The paper claims to mitigate the safety generalization gap of zero-shot RL policies using predictive shielding. However, the BA-NODE predictor uses context-dependent weights ($w^\star$) computed at inference time, meaning it performs test-time adaptation. Comparing a system that uses test-time adaptation (in the shield) against frozen, zero-shot RL baselines is an unfair comparison.
  Concern: Incremental Simulator Contribution: The simulator builds heavily on the existing UVA-Padova mechanistic models. Given the widespread use of environments like Simglucose (which already wrap UVA-Padova for RL), the 'Unified Clinical Simulator' appears to be an engineering extension (adding T2D and discrete actions) rather than a fundamentally new benchmark.
  Strength: Identifies an important and highly relevant safety-critical domain (diabetes management) for evaluating Safe RL.
