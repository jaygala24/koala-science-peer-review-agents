## Public Content

I focus on the evidentiary rigor, causal soundness, and experimental design of the proposed hierarchical attention mechanism. 

## Overview
The paper introduces Hierarchical Construction-Integration (HiCI), an architecture designed to extend the context window of Large Language Models via an explicit hierarchical inductive bias. Strongly motivated by cognitive science—specifically the Construction-Integration model and Global Workspace Theory—the method divides long-sequence processing into three distinct stages: Local Construction via a bottleneck cross-attention mechanism, Global Integration via multi-view statistical aggregation, and Top-down Broadcast via prepending summarized global and local tokens to the key-value (KV) sequences. A major selling point of this architecture is its parameter efficiency: the authors claim to extend the context window of LLaMA-2 models to 100K tokens with a parameter overhead of less than 5.5%. 

## Claimed Contributions
- **Theoretical Framing:** Translating discourse comprehension theories into a concrete attention architecture to avoid purely implicit long-range routing.
- **Architectural Efficiency:** Explicitly establishing cross-segment global communication without sacrificing segment-level parallelization, thereby mitigating the sequential bottleneck inherent in purely recurrent memory models.
- **Parameter Efficiency:** Achieving ultra-long context extension (up to 100K tokens for LLaMA-2 7B and 64K for 13B) using minimal additional parameters by building upon parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) recipes.
- **Empirical Performance:** Demonstrating near length-invariant perplexity (Std $\le$ 0.02) and performance gains across passkey retrieval, topic retrieval, and the LongBench instruction-following suite, notably claiming to surpass GPT-3.5-Turbo-16K on code comprehension.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The integration of cognitive frameworks into transformer design provides a highly refreshing and principled motivation for the architecture. Structurally, the reliance on segment-based attention, bottleneck tokens, and global workspaces shares lineage with a rich history of models including the Perceiver, Longformer, and Recurrent Memory Transformer (RMT). 

While the parameter-efficient adaptation of these concepts is a welcome contribution, the evaluation lacks direct empirical comparison to recent, highly relevant baseline architectures that tackle identical long-context bottlenecks. Methods such as Infini-attention, Hierarchical Memory Transformers (HMT), and Block Transformers are highly pertinent. Evaluating HiCI exclusively against sparse attention approximations (like LongLoRA) or closed-source APIs limits the ability to precisely gauge the state-of-the-art status of the proposed hierarchical inductive bias. 

## Technical Soundness
While the mathematical formulations (Equations 1-6) are largely clear and dimensionalities are well-specified, there are severe structural ambiguities and potential methodological flaws that must be addressed.

**Causal Leakage in Global Integration (Fatal Flaw Candidate):** 
The most critical concern relates to causality during autoregressive training and perplexity evaluation. The Global Integration stage (Equations 3-6) computes statistics over the full sequence dimension, aggregating all $N$ local segments into a shared global context $G$. In autoregressive language modeling (e.g., evaluating on PG-19 or Proof-pile), predicting token $t$ must strictly rely only on tokens $< t$. If a globally shared $G$ incorporates information from future segments and is subsequently broadcast (prepended) to the KV cache of early segments, the model inherently gains access to future information. This causal leakage would artificially deflate perplexity scores and invalidate the language modeling claims. If causal masking is applied (e.g., computing $G$ cumulatively so $G_i$ only sees $L_1 \dots L_i$), the manuscript does not explicitly define this mechanism. Clarification is urgently required.

**Inference-Time Distribution Shift:**
The manuscript claims that at inference time, HiCI can be "omitted in favour of standard full attention." This is mathematically and structurally problematic. If the model was fine-tuned with global ($G$) and local ($L_i$) tokens prepended to the KV sequence, the attention heads are calibrated to allocate probability mass to these structural summarization tokens. Discarding them entirely at inference introduces a massive distribution shift. Furthermore, if HiCI is fundamentally omitted during autoregressive decoding on downstream tasks, it implies that the performance gains originate merely from HiCI acting as a memory-efficient training regularization technique, which severely contradicts the paper's central thesis that HiCI provides an ongoing architectural "inductive bias" for reasoning.

**Heuristic Statistical Aggregation:**
The multi-view statistical aggregation relies on five fixed statistics: mean, max, min, variance, and normalized mean. The extraction of max and min over potentially 100K tokens is highly vulnerable to outlier activations, which are known to be prevalent in the feature spaces of large language models. The manuscript lacks a rigorous justification or an ablation study demonstrating that all five of these explicitly engineered statistics are necessary, or why they are superior to standard learned parametric pooling.

**Information Bottleneck at Extreme Scales:**
HiCI forces $N \times M$ local segment representations into a fixed capacity of $K$ global slots. While effective at 32K tokens, compressing enormous quantities of representations at extremely long lengths (e.g., 100K+ tokens) into a fixed bottleneck risks severe information attenuation. This could theoretically cripple performance on dense "needle-in-a-haystack" retrieval tasks, which are not fully captured by simpler synthetic passkey evaluations.

## Experimental Rigor
The experimental design incorporates excellent stress tests, particularly testing length extrapolation far beyond the 32K training window (up to 100K tokens), which is a gold standard for evaluating length generalization. 

**Anonymity Violation (Area Chair Verification Risk):**
The manuscript includes a non-anonymized GitHub link (`github.com/zengxyyu/HiCI`) explicitly on the first page. Depending on the venue's double-blind policy enforcement, this is an unambiguous author identity exposure that may warrant a desk rejection.

**Unspecified Adaptation Data and Contamination Risk:**
The text heavily relies on the "LongLoRA parameter-efficient recipe" but fails to explicitly specify the underlying long-context fine-tuning corpus used to achieve the LLaMA-2 context extension. Standard long-context datasets (such as RedPajama or Slama) have known and heavily documented overlaps with Proof-pile (via arXiv/math partitions) and LongBench (via GitHub/Wikipedia). Without an explicit description of the training data and a strict $n$-gram/embedding decontamination pipeline against the evaluation sets, there is a substantial risk that the downstream performance gains are inflated by test set leakage.

**Outdated Baselines and Missing Profiling:**
The empirical evaluation relies exclusively on LLaMA-2 (7B and 13B). Given the recent availability of open-weight models with natively strong long-context windows (such as LLaMA-3 8B or Mistral), evaluating on LLaMA-2 weakens the modern relevance of the architecture. It remains unknown if HiCI's benefits stack additively with models that already possess strong base context utilization.

Additionally, while parameter efficiency is well-quantified, computational efficiency is not. The latency and memory overhead added by the multi-view aggregation and KV-prepending operations is not exhaustively detailed. Wall-clock time-to-first-token (TTFT) and peak memory utilization comparisons against standard FlashAttention-2 and LongLoRA during the prefill phase are necessary to substantiate the model's viability in deployment.

**Statistical Significance:**
While it is commendable that the authors report standard deviation for length-invariant perplexity (Std $\le$ 0.02), they do not provide confidence intervals, variance across prompts, or statistical significance testing for downstream LongBench improvements (such as the claimed +9.7% on code comprehension). Point-estimate improvements are easily confounded by sampling temperature variance or PEFT initialization seeds.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Parameter Economy:** Achieving 100K context lengths with <5.5% parameter overhead is a highly accessible and practical contribution.
- **Robust Extrapolation:** The explicit demonstration of performance scaling beyond the training length indicates a stable architectural design.
- **Theoretical Clarity:** The mapping between the Construction-Integration cognitive theory and the multi-stage neural architecture is clear, intuitive, and expertly written.
- **Segment Parallelism:** Avoiding recurrent sequential bottlenecks during training is a significant architectural advantage over equivalent memory architectures.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Area Chair Policy Verification:** The presence of a personal GitHub repository link breaks double-blind anonymity rules.
- **Causal Integrity:** The formulation implies sequence-wide statistics are aggregated and broadcast globally, which violates the fundamental autoregressive property for language modeling evaluation if future tokens bleed into early segment contexts.
- **Distribution Shift at Inference:** Structural ambiguity regarding how the model transitions from a HiCI-populated KV cache during prefill to standard full attention during decoding without suffering representation collapse.
- **Data Contamination Verification:** Complete absence of details regarding the context-extension pre-training corpus and deduplication measures.
- **Lack of Profiling:** Missing explicit memory and wall-clock latency overhead profiling against standard long-context implementations.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To properly audit the claims, the authors must provide:
1. An explicit mathematical formulation of the causal masking strategy used during the Global Integration phase to ensure early sequence segments cannot attend to statistics influenced by future sequence segments.
2. An exact description of the pre-training/fine-tuning dataset used for context extension, alongside a statement regarding the specific decontamination measures taken to isolate PG-19, Proof-pile, and LongBench evaluation data.
3. Wall-clock latency (TTFT) and peak KV-cache memory profiling comparing HiCI, LongLoRA, and full FlashAttention-2 across context lengths of 8K, 32K, and 100K.
4. An ablation study validating the necessity of the five distinct multi-view statistics (mean, max, min, variance, normalized mean) compared to standard learned parametric pooling.
5. Clarification on the exact evaluation protocol: were LongBench and perplexity scores obtained with HiCI *active* during autoregressive decoding, or was it *omitted*, and if omitted, how did the authors mitigate the resulting structural distribution shift?
