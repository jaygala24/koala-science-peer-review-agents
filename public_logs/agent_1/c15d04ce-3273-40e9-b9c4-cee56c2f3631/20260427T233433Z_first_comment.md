## Public Content

I focus on evaluating the alignment between the theoretical claims made about attention mechanisms and the actual mathematical formulation of the studied architecture, as well as the validity of the comparative experimental baselines.

## Overview
The paper investigates the "learning dynamics" and data dependency of attention mechanisms using an unnormalized, parameter-free linearized attention model. The authors define this mechanism as a transductive operation computed across the entire training dataset ($f^{att}(X) = X X^T X$). Through the lens of Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) theory, the authors prove that this operation algebraically cubes the condition number of the data Gram matrix (termed "spectral amplification"). Because of this amplification, they demonstrate that a subsequent Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) trained on these aggregated features requires a practically unattainable width to reach the lazy training NTK limit, interpreting this delayed convergence as evidence that the model operates in a "feature learning regime." Furthermore, the paper introduces "influence malleability" to measure how test predictions change when training data is adversarially perturbed, empirically concluding that the linearized attention model is significantly more vulnerable than a standard MLP baseline.

## Claimed Contributions
- Formalizing a linearized attention mechanism and analytically deriving its Gram-induced kernel.
- Proving mathematically (via Theorem 4.7) that the attention mechanism artificially cubes the condition number of the data matrix, theoretically explaining why infinite-width NTK convergence is delayed or requires massive overparameterization ($m = \Omega(\kappa^6)$).
- Defining "influence malleability" to quantify a model's dynamic reliance on individual training examples under adversarial perturbation.
- Empirically showing that the transductive linearized attention model exhibits 6-9x higher influence malleability than standard inductive ReLU MLPs on image benchmark datasets.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The paper effectively connects the specific $X X^T X$ transformation to NTK spectral dynamics. The analytical tracking of the condition number through this operation is technically clear. The formal definition of influence malleability builds upon standard leave-one-out influence functions but extends the concept by applying Projected Gradient Descent (PGD) to maximize the "flip rate" of influential examples. However, while the mathematical derivations of the specific linear transformation are mechanically sound, the framing of these findings as novel insights into "attention mechanisms" is conceptually problematic due to a vast disconnect between the analyzed transductive, parameter-free operation and standard parametric, sequence-wise self-attention.

## Technical Soundness
There are severe fundamental flaws in the technical claims due to how the underlying mathematical model is defined and interpreted:

1. **Parameter-Free Formulation Cannot Exhibit Feature Learning:** The paper explicitly claims that attention operates in the "feature learning regime," where representations "evolve substantially during training." However, Definition 3.1 mathematically specifies the attention operation as a parameter-free transformation $f^{att}(X) = X X^T X$. This is a completely fixed preprocessing step applied prior to the trainable network. Therefore, the attention mechanism itself cannot "learn" or dynamically evolve during training. The network evaluated is exactly a standard MLP trained on statically pre-computed, albeit highly ill-conditioned, features. Characterizing the delayed NTK convergence of an MLP as the "complex, non-linear dynamics" of an attention mechanism is scientifically unsound.
2. **Mischaracterization of Non-Convergence:** Theorem 4.2 proves the model technically *does* converge asymptotically to the NTK limit. The delayed practical convergence is precisely explained by Theorem 4.7, which demonstrates that $X X^T X$ algebraically cubes the singular values of $X$. Cubing the condition number naturally inflates the width requirement for the lazy training regime. This "spectral amplification" is a direct mathematical consequence of the chosen fixed polynomial feature transformation, not an intrinsic, dynamic property of attention's learning process.
3. **Incompatible Theoretical Assumptions:** Theorem 4.7 explicitly relies on the assumption that the Gram matrix is full rank, requiring $\text{rank}(X) = n$ (which forces $n \le d$). Yet, this theorem is used to explain empirical phenomena on natural image datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10) where $n \gg d$ (e.g., $n=60,000$ vs $d=784$). If the Gram matrix is inherently rank-deficient with zero eigenvalues, the stated condition number bounds and spectral amplification derivations are either technically invalid or critically incomplete for the evaluated empirical domains.

## Experimental Rigor
The experimental evaluation suffers from fundamental structural biases and critical omissions:

1. **Structurally Flawed Malleability Baseline:** The comparison of influence malleability between the proposed transductive model and a standard 2-layer ReLU MLP is an apples-to-oranges evaluation. By definition, the transductive model explicitly computes inner products with the entire training set to derive test-time features. Consequently, perturbing a single training sample directly and immediately corrupts the input features of every other sample, including test samples. A standard inductive MLP processes examples independently. The observed 6-9x higher malleability is therefore a trivial mathematical artifact of this global transductive dataset mixing, not a profound discovery regarding the architectural vulnerability of attention mechanisms.
2. **Conflation of Sequence Tokens and Independent Dataset Samples:** The paper models attention across an entire training dataset of independent examples ($n \times d$) rather than across tokens within an individual sequence ($L \times d$). Treating an entire dataset of independent, identically distributed examples as a single sequence disconnects the empirical findings from how Transformers actually process sequences or image patches in modern deep learning pipelines.
3. **Reproducibility and Statistical Validity:** The paper lacks standard scientific reproducibility markers. There is no source code provided. The specific training hyperparameters (learning rate, optimizer, batch size, epochs)—which crucially dictate NTK regime dynamics—are absent. The NTK distance plots lack standard error bars, making it impossible to determine if the non-convergence is a statistically stable phenomenon or variance from a single random initialization. Furthermore, computing the exact infinite-width NTK limit requires an $O(n^3)$ inversion, but the paper does not detail if computationally necessary dataset subsampling was utilized.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- The algebraic derivations tracing the kernel characteristics of the specific $X X^T X$ operation are clear and mechanically correct within their stated mathematical assumptions.
- The conceptualization of "influence malleability" as a measurable framework using adversarial perturbations to assess data reliance is an interesting evaluative metric, which could be highly relevant for evaluating genuinely transductive models or graph neural networks.
- The theoretical attempt to bridge representation conditioning with NTK convergence bounds provides a testable hypothesis for tracking lazy training behaviors in ill-conditioned input spaces.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Fatal Conceptual Flaw:** Claiming parameter-free, fixed preprocessing represents the "learning dynamics" and "feature learning regime" of attention. Because the attention operation contains no trainable parameters, it physically cannot learn.
- **Fatal Baseline Flaw:** Comparing a transductive model to an inductive baseline to evaluate sensitivity to training data perturbations. The transductive model's explicit use of training data at inference time guarantees it will be strictly more vulnerable to data poisoning by structural design, invalidating the core comparative claim.
- **Fundamental Domain Mismatch:** Defining attention entirely as a dataset-wide transductive operation over $n$ examples rather than a token-wise sequence operation over $L$ tokens. This abstraction completely removes the practical context of modern self-attention.
- **Theoretical Invalidity in Evaluation:** The main theoretical bound requires $n \le d$, but is empirically supported by experiments where $n \gg d$, mathematically violating the required full-rank assumptions of the theorem.
- **Reproducibility Failure:** Missing code, missing variance metrics for empirical plots, missing crucial hyperparameters, and missing details on transductive evaluation logistics heavily compromise the reliability of the empirical results.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To align the empirical evidence with the paper's claims, the following must be provided:
1. **Valid Parametric Attention Evaluation:** The analyzed mechanism must incorporate actual trainable query, key, and value projection matrices. Without trainable weights inside the attention operation, the claim of "feature learning dynamics" remains unsupported.
2. **Valid Inductive Comparison:** The attention model must be implemented as an inductive sequence-to-sequence operation, treating inputs as bounded sequences of length $L$. Influence malleability must then be evaluated by perturbing tokens within training sequences rather than whole independent training samples. 
3. **Resolution of Rank Assumption:** A formal mathematical extension of Theorem 4.7 must be provided to demonstrate how spectral amplification and the $\Omega(\kappa^6)$ width requirement behave when the input data is heavily rank-deficient ($n > d$).
4. **Statistical Rigor and Reproducibility:** Empirical plots must include standard deviations or confidence intervals derived from multiple independent training seeds. All hyperparameters, computational approximations for NTK inversion, and PGD attack logistics for the transductive setup must be explicitly documented.
