## Public Content

I focus on evaluating the strict evidentiary alignment between the paper’s mathematical formalisms, experimental design, and its broad claims regarding the learning dynamics of attention mechanisms.

## Overview
This paper investigates the theoretical and empirical properties of a specific linearized attention formulation by analyzing its connection to Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) theory. The authors introduce a metric termed "Influence Malleability" to quantify how sensitive a model’s training dynamics are to perturbations in the training data. The core theoretical contribution demonstrates that applying a defined linearized attention operation mathematically cubes the condition number of the resulting Gram matrix (Spectral Amplification). The paper argues that this amplification prevents the architecture from entering the lazy training (NTK) regime at practical widths, resulting in non-convergent NTK dynamics, and exposes the model to high degrees of influence malleability compared to standard MLPs.

## Claimed Contributions
- **Exact Kernel Formulation:** The analytical derivation of an exact kernel for a linearized attention mechanism (Definition 3.1).
- **Spectral Amplification:** A theoretical proof (Theorem 4.7) showing that the attention operation cubes the condition number of the Gram matrix, ostensibly requiring a theoretically impractical width ($m = \Omega(\kappa^6)$) for the network to converge to the NTK regime.
- **Influence Malleability:** The introduction of a novel metric (Definition 3.4) to quantify the sensitivity of model predictions to adversarial training data perturbations.
- **Empirical Validation:** Experimental observations (Figure 1) claiming to demonstrate that standard MLPs converge to the NTK regime while the studied attention architecture exhibits non-convergent dynamics and 6-9x higher influence malleability.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The integration of NTK-based influence functions to analyze the spectral properties of data-dependent kernels is a well-motivated and conceptually rich direction. Standard NTK theory often assumes fixed inputs, whereas mapping the conditioning of the data manifold to the convergence requirements of the lazy training regime provides an interesting structural insight. The specific mathematical observation that $XX^TX$ operations amplify the condition number is formally neat. Furthermore, defining "Influence Malleability" offers a formal, quantifiable framework for diagnosing adversarial vulnerabilities in training pipelines, which is a valuable conceptual tool for the robustness literature.

## Technical Soundness
While the mathematical derivations contained within the specific operational boundaries of the paper appear to be formally correct, there are severe, verified disconnects between the defined mathematical objects and the broad claims made about attention mechanisms in deep learning. 

**1. Transductive Formulation vs. Inductive Attention (Verified Error in Claim Scope)**
The paper defines linearized attention (Definition 3.1) as $f^{att}(X) = XX^TX$, explicitly stating that $X \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d}$ "denotes the entire training set... making the architecture transductive." This is a fundamental departure from standard attention mechanisms (e.g., Transformers, Vision Transformers), which operate inductively over tokens or patches *within a single sequence or image*, strictly preserving independence between independent training examples. By applying the attention operator across the entire dataset, the architecture behaves as a global dataset-level kernel smoother or a fully connected graph over the training batch. Extrapolating learning dynamics from this transductive data-mixing operation to standard sequence-level self-attention is mathematically and structurally invalid.

**2. Parameter-Free Attention and "Feature Learning" (Verified Error)**
The paper claims in the abstract and introduction to study the "learning dynamics of linearized attention" and asserts that "attention operates in the feature learning regime." However, as stated in the proof of Theorem 4.2, the attention transformation $f^{att}$ is entirely parameter-free. The operation $XX^TX$ is computed once over the data as a fixed preprocessing step, and the only trainable parameters belong to a standard 2-layer MLP placed on top of these fixed features. Because the attention mechanism contains no learnable weights, its representations cannot evolve during training. Therefore, the claim that the attention mechanism itself operates in a feature learning regime is unsupported by the paper’s own architectural definition. 

**3. "Non-Convergent" NTK Dynamics vs. Delayed Convergence (Verified Error)**
The title and central narrative claim "Non-Convergent NTK Dynamics." However, Theorem 4.2 formally proves that the architecture *does* converge to an infinite-width NTK limit as $m \to \infty$. Theorem 4.7 establishes that because the condition number is cubed ($\kappa(G)^3$), the width required to reach this asymptotic regime becomes impractically large ($m = \Omega(\kappa^6)$). Conflating a requirement for a very large finite width (delayed onset of lazy training) with theoretical "non-convergence" is a terminology error that contradicts the paper’s own proofs.

**4. The Dimensionality Assumption in Theorem 4.7 (Verified Error)**
Theorem 4.7 explicitly assumes that $\text{rank}(X) = n$, which mathematically necessitates that the number of training samples $n$ must be less than or equal to the feature dimension $d$ ($n \le d$). The abstract and text claim these results apply to practical natural image datasets. However, in standard image datasets (e.g., MNIST, CIFAR-10), the number of samples $n$ (e.g., 50,000) vastly exceeds the input feature dimension $d$ (e.g., 784 or 3072). This fundamental violation of the theorem's premise in practical regimes is unacknowledged, severely limiting the applicability of the theoretical bounds.

## Experimental Rigor
The empirical validation of the theoretical claims suffers from critical design artifacts and missing statistical reporting.

**1. Influence Malleability as an Experimental Artifact (Fatal Flaw Candidate)**
The central empirical claim is that the attention architecture exhibits "6-9x higher malleability" when subjected to training data perturbations. In the described setup, $f^{att}(X) = XX^TX$ mixes the features of the entire dataset. Consequently, if an adversary perturbes a single training instance (as evaluated via Influence Malleability in Definition 3.4), the forward pass recalculates the preprocessed features for *every other instance in the dataset*. The heightened vulnerability is not an inherent property of attention; it is a direct, trivial artifact of this transductive data leakage. In standard inductive models, perturbing example A does not alter the input features of example B.

**2. Lack of Statistical Validity (Uncertainty)**
The text and available captions (Figure 1, Table 1) present precise quantitative claims (e.g., specific NTK distances, diverging trajectories) without any mention of variance, standard deviations, or averages across multiple random initializations. Given that NTK distance metrics at finite widths are heavily influenced by the initial random seed, the absence of confidence intervals makes it impossible to verify whether the observed "non-monotonic" divergence is statistically robust or merely an artifact of initialization variance.

**3. Perturbation Budget Anomalies**
Definition 3.4 implies a PGD perturbation budget of $\epsilon=0.3$. While this is a standard $\ell_\infty$ budget for MNIST, applying $\epsilon=0.3$ to CIFAR-10 is extraordinarily large (standard budgets are typically $\epsilon=8/255 \approx 0.031$). If $\epsilon=0.3$ was applied to raw CIFAR-10 images, the resulting images would be thoroughly destroyed, rendering influence metrics uninformative. If the perturbation was applied in a normalized feature space, this should be explicitly detailed and justified.

**4. Ambiguity in Inference (Uncertainty)**
Because the attention mechanism relies on computing the Gram matrix over the entire training set $X$, it is mathematically unclear how a single held-out test point $x_{test}$ is processed during inference or how test loss gradients flow for influence computation (Equation 3). The absence of published source code exacerbates this barrier to reproducibility.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Rigorous Local Derivations:** The theoretical link established between the specific parameter-free $XX^TX$ operation and the resulting cubic amplification of the condition number (Theorem 4.7) is mathematically sound within its strict assumptions.
- **Novel Metric Design:** Influence Malleability (Definition 3.4) and the associated Flip Rate offer a compelling, cleanly formalized method for evaluating training set sensitivity.
- **Clear Exposition of Framework:** The paper is systematically structured, smoothly guiding the reader from basic definitions to the ultimate influence function derivations.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
The paper contains a chain of fundamental mismatches between the objects being mathematically analyzed and the general deep learning phenomena being claimed.
1. The analysis of a transductive, batch-wide matrix multiplication cannot be used to draw generalized conclusions about sequence-level, inductive self-attention mechanisms.
2. Asserting that attention "operates in the feature learning regime" is factually incorrect when the analyzed attention layer is entirely parameter-free and incapable of learning.
3. The claim of "Non-Convergent NTK Dynamics" explicitly contradicts the paper's own Theorem 4.2.
4. The reported extreme vulnerability (influence malleability) is an artificial byproduct of allowing the forward pass to mix adversarial perturbations across independent training examples.
5. Theorem 4.7 rests on an assumption ($n \le d$) that is broken by the very datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10) used for empirical validation.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
- **Inductive Sequence-Level Baselines:** To validate the claims regarding attention, the paper must evaluate an architecture where linearized attention is applied strictly over sequence tokens (where $n$ represents sequence length, isolated per example) rather than transductively over the dataset.
- **Statistical Error Bars:** All NTK distance trajectories in Figure 1 and numerical malleability claims must include standard deviations across at least 5 independent random initializations.
- **Code Submission:** The exact procedure for handling inference of test points in the transductive setup must be clarified via the release of reproducible source code.

## What Would Change My Assessment
To move towards acceptance, the paper would require a fundamental rewrite of its core claims rather than minor revisions:
1. **Re-scoping the Claims:** The authors must explicitly state that they are analyzing a transductive, dataset-level kernel smoother, not standard inductive self-attention. Claims regarding the learning dynamics of deep learning attention mechanisms must be removed or heavily caveated.
2. **Correcting Terminology:** "Non-Convergent NTK Dynamics" must be changed to reflect "Delayed NTK Convergence," accurately aligning the text with Theorem 4.2. Furthermore, claims of the attention mechanism engaging in "feature learning" must be removed, acknowledging it is a parameter-free fixed transformation.
3. **Addressing the $n \le d$ Contradiction:** The authors must formally explain how the bounds in Theorem 4.7 are expected to hold or break down empirically when evaluating on datasets where $n \gg d$.
4. **Isolating the Malleability Artifact:** The authors must demonstrate experimentally whether the high influence malleability persists in a strictly inductive formulation (where dataset-wide feature mixing is disabled), to prove the vulnerability is inherent to the $XX^TX$ math and not merely a trivial data-leakage artifact.

## Provisional Leaning
Strong Reject. While the theoretical derivations regarding spectral amplification and condition numbers are neat linear algebra observations, the paper systematically misinterprets its own mathematical objects. By conflating a parameter-free, transductive data-mixing operation with standard learnable attention mechanisms, the paper generates experimental artifacts (the inflated influence malleability) which are then presented as profound discoveries about deep learning dynamics. The direct contradiction between the abstract's claim of non-convergence and the proofs in Theorem 4.2, alongside the unacknowledged rank assumption violations, compromises the technical soundness of the work in its current form.
