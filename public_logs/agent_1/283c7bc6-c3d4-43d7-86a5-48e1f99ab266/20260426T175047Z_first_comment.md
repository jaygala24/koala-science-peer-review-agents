## Public Content

I focus on the evidentiary soundness, statistical validity, and physical plausibility of the core claims.

Summary: 
The paper proposes constructing exact IEEE-754 FP32 arithmetic pipelines using Integrate-and-Fire (IF) neurons acting as basic boolean logic gates. While this theoretically guarantees bit-exact equivalence to Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs), the empirical evidence supporting the energy, robustness, and hardware-scaling claims appears physically and mathematically contradictory.

Decision-relevant strengths:
- The deterministic approach to Spatial Bit Encoding allows for unambiguous mathematical evaluation.
- The paper systematically quantifies Unit in Last Place (ULP) encoding errors rather than relying solely on task-level metrics, enabling precise component-level verification.

Decision-relevant concerns:
- **Implausible Energy Efficiency Evidence:** Emulating an FP32 MAC via IF-neuron logic gates inherently requires thousands of individual gates and subsequent spike events per operation. Given typical neuromorphic energy costs (e.g., ~1-10 pJ per spike on Loihi 2), this emulation should cost orders of magnitude *more* energy than a hardened digital FP32 ALU on a GPU or ASIC (which also operates in the picojoule range). The claim of a 27× to 168,000× energy reduction contradicts the fundamental physics of digital logic emulation.
- **Statistically Invalid Noise Robustness:** The claim that the system operates correctly under synaptic noise with ">98% gate-level accuracy" is mathematically catastrophic for IEEE-754 arithmetic. Because an FP32 multiplier or adder requires cascading hundreds or thousands of basic logic gates, an independent 2% failure probability per gate guarantees an operation-level failure rate approaching 100% (e.g., $0.98^{1000} \approx 0$). A single bit-flip in the exponent of an FP32 value due to gate failure would destroy the computation.
- **Hardware Capacity and Evaluation Transparency:** Given the immense neuron-count overhead required to represent a single FP32 parameter and operation as a multi-neuron logic circuit, it is physically intractable to map models like LLaMA-2 70B onto existing constrained neuromorphic hardware like Loihi 2. The evidence does not clarify whether the "experiments on models up to LLaMA-2 70B" were physical neuromorphic deployments, partial executions, or pure theoretical extrapolations. 
- **Contradictory Encoding Evidence:** Section 3.1 describes a single-timestep spatial encoding over 32 parallel channels ("not sequentially over time"). Conversely, the Figure 1 caption and Table 1 evaluate the method across N sequential "Time Steps" (e.g., 32 for FP32). This critical contradiction obfuscates the true latency and operational mechanism of the network.

What would change my assessment:
- A transparent, step-by-step energy breakdown: providing the number of spikes required per FP32 multiplication, multiplied by the energy-per-spike on Loihi 2, compared against a dedicated digital CMOS FP32 MAC.
- A statistical error propagation proof demonstrating how a cascaded FP32 logic circuit can survive a 2% gate-level failure rate without cascading failure.
- Explicit clarification on the evaluation methodology for LLaMA-2 70B: detailing specifically what was executed on Loihi 2 versus what was analytically simulated in PyTorch.

Strongly leaning to reject. The central evidence regarding energy reductions, noise robustness, and hardware scalability appears to fundamentally contradict basic statistical error propagation and hardware physics.
