## Overview
The paper introduces Directional Concentration Uncertainty (DCU), an embedding-based framework designed to quantify uncertainty in Large Language Models (LLMs) and multi-modal generative models. The core premise is to replace the computationally expensive, discrete clustering steps of existing methods—such as Semantic Entropy, which relies heavily on $O(N^2)$ bidirectional entailment checks—with a continuous measure of geometric dispersion. By mapping multiple sampled generations from a language model into a continuous embedding space (e.g., using the `e5-large-v2` model) and projecting them onto a unit hypersphere, the authors model the distribution of these semantic representations using a von Mises-Fisher (vMF) distribution. The paper proposes using the inverse of the vMF concentration parameter ($\kappa^{-1}$) as the primary proxy for generative uncertainty, hypothetically allowing for scalable, black-box uncertainty quantification that can generalize to multi-modal tasks.

## Claimed Contributions
- **Continuous Uncertainty Metric:** Proposes DCU, which uses the inverse concentration parameter ($\kappa^{-1}$) of a fitted von Mises-Fisher distribution to quantify the semantic dispersion of LLM outputs.
- **Computational Efficiency:** Eliminates the need for discrete clustering and rigid natural language inference (NLI) entailment heuristics, bypassing the quadratic computational cost of bidirectional entailment required by standard Semantic Entropy.
- **Empirical Superiority:** Claims in the abstract that DCU "matches or exceeds calibration levels of prior works" across unimodal and multimodal tasks.
- **Modality Agnosticism:** Asserts that the approach generalizes easily to multimodal datasets (such as ScienceQA) where text-only NLI models typically fail.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The conceptual transition from discrete entailment-based uncertainty to continuous embedding-based geometric dispersion is an intuitive and highly relevant direction for the field of LLM uncertainty quantification (UQ). Discarding ad-hoc thresholds and clustering heuristics in favor of a theoretically grounded directional statistics framework (the vMF distribution) is an elegant proposition. 

However, the novelty of the specific statistical procedure proposed is severely undermined by its mathematical equivalence to standard baselines, which is not acknowledged in the text. Furthermore, the use of dense representations for semantic similarity is well-explored, and prior works have utilized embedding variance or semantic density for uncertainty. The primary novelty here is strictly the formulation of this dispersion via the vMF maximum likelihood estimation (MLE), a formulation that unfortunately introduces severe statistical and practical limitations, detailed below.

## Technical Soundness
There are profound theoretical, mathematical, and statistical flaws in the proposed methodology that significantly compromise the soundness of the paper.

**1. Obfuscation of a Trivial Baseline via Rank Equivalence**
The paper formulates the uncertainty metric as the inverse of the concentration parameter, $\kappa^{-1}$. The parameter $\kappa$ is estimated via Maximum Likelihood Estimation by solving the equation $A_d(\kappa) = \bar{R}$, where $\bar{R}$ is the empirical mean resultant length of the generated embeddings on the hypersphere, and $A_d(\kappa)$ is the ratio of modified Bessel functions of the first kind $I_{d/2}(\kappa) / I_{d/2-1}(\kappa)$. 
Crucially, $A_d(\kappa)$ is a strictly monotonically increasing function of $\kappa$. Consequently, the estimated $\kappa$ is a strictly monotonically increasing function of $\bar{R}$, meaning $\kappa^{-1}$ is a strictly monotonically decreasing function of $\bar{R}$. 
Because $\bar{R}$ is directly proportional to the average pairwise cosine similarity of the embeddings, the proposed DCU metric ($\kappa^{-1}$) is perfectly rank-equivalent to the negative average cosine similarity of the outputs. Standard UQ evaluations measure calibration via rank-based or sorting-based metrics, such as Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic (AUROC), Area Under the Accuracy-Rejection Curve (AUARC), or Expected Calibration Error (ECE) via monotonic binning. For any of these downstream evaluation metrics, DCU will yield identical performance to simply calculating the average pairwise cosine similarity. Framing this procedure as a novel vMF Maximum Likelihood Estimation requiring numerical Newton-Raphson solvers artificially inflates the technical contribution and obfuscates the fact that the method is functionally identical to a trivial, non-parametric baseline.

**2. Severe Small-Sample, High-Dimensional Statistical Bias**
The statistical formulation is extremely ill-posed given the regime in which it operates. The paper relies on pre-trained embedding models, such as `e5-large-v2`, which have high dimensionality (e.g., $d=1024$). Simultaneously, Figure 1 indicates that the method samples $N=10$ generations. Estimating the concentration parameter of a 1024-dimensional vMF distribution using Maximum Likelihood Estimation on only 10 samples is statistically fraught. It is a well-documented statistical phenomenon that the MLE for the vMF concentration parameter $\kappa$ is severely biased upward when the sample size $N$ is small, and this bias is violently exacerbated in high dimensions ($N \ll d$). The manuscript provides no high-dimensional bias-correction factor or regularization, meaning the estimated $\kappa$ values will be highly unstable and dominated by high-dimensional noise rather than true semantic dispersion.

**3. Embedding Anisotropy and Factual vs. Topical Similarity**
The framework implicitly assumes that geometric distance in a continuous embedding space aligns perfectly with semantic entailment. This assumption fails on two fronts:
*   *Anisotropy:* Pre-trained text embeddings are notoriously anisotropic (the "representation degradation" or "narrow cone" effect). The vMF distribution assumes isotropic dispersion around a mean direction. Fitting an isotropic vMF model to a highly anisotropic embedding space convolutes the LLM's uncertainty with the geometric artifacts of the specific embedding model.
*   *Topical vs. Factual Similarity:* Contrastive embedding models like `e5` often group sentences by structural and topical similarity rather than factual equivalence. For instance, "The capital is Paris" and "The capital is London" are factually contradictory, and an NLI model (used in Semantic Entropy) correctly flags them as such. However, an embedding model will assign them an extremely high cosine similarity because they share the exact same syntactic structure and domain. DCU would therefore compute a high $\bar{R}$, resulting in a high $\kappa$ and erroneously reporting low uncertainty for contradictory factual hallucinations.

## Experimental Rigor
The experimental rigor of the submission cannot be fully evaluated because **the manuscript is severely incomplete.** 

**1. Fatal Flaw: Missing Results Section**
The text abruptly cuts off mid-sentence in Section 4.1 (Datasets and Sampling Strategy): *"...which gives us the opportunity to evaluate on (i) a more challenging task, given the multimodal aspect, and (ii) a da"*. Absolutely no experimental results, quantitative metrics, charts, tables, or discussions are provided in the available text. Consequently, the central claims made in the abstract—specifically that DCU "matches or exceeds calibration levels of prior works"—are entirely unsupported by any empirical evidence. 

**2. High Risk of Benchmark Contamination and Data Leakage**
Based on the experimental setup described in the truncated text, the authors intend to evaluate DCU on standard NLP benchmarks: SQuAD, TriviaQA, and NQ-Open. Simultaneously, they state they are using the `e5-large-v2` model to generate embeddings. The `e5` family of models is heavily fine-tuned using supervised contrastive learning on massive datasets, explicitly including standard QA datasets like MS-MARCO, SQuAD, NQ, and TriviaQA. 
Evaluating an embedding-based uncertainty metric on the exact supervised training datasets used to align the embedding model introduces a severe data leakage risk. The embedding space has been explicitly optimized to cluster semantically related answers for these specific benchmark questions. Therefore, evaluating DCU on SQuAD using `e5-large-v2` does not measure the zero-shot semantic dispersion of the evaluated generative model; rather, it measures the supervised optimization artifacts of the embedding model's training mixture. This invalidates the claim that the method requires no task-specific heuristics.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Elegant Motivation:** The goal of replacing computationally expensive bidirectional NLI clustering with a continuous vector-space dispersion measure is highly practically relevant. If successful, it would significantly lower the barrier to deploying rigorous UQ for production LLMs.
- **Multimodal Ambition:** Attempting to extend uncertainty quantification to multimodal settings (like ScienceQA) addresses a significant gap in the current literature, where image-text entailment remains immature compared to text-to-text NLI.
- **Well-Formatted Submissions:** The visible parts of the manuscript strictly adhere to the double-blind formatting requirements and clearly outline the limitations of prior discrete heuristics.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Incomplete Submission:** The most immediate fatal flaw is the truncation of the paper in Section 4.1. A paper cannot be accepted without its results, baseline comparisons, ablations, and conclusions.
- **Mathematical Triviality:** The proposed $\kappa^{-1}$ metric offers no rank-based expressive power over a simple calculation of the negative average pairwise cosine similarity of the embeddings.
- **Statistical Invalidity:** Using an uncorrected MLE to estimate a 1024-dimensional vMF distribution from 10 samples is statistically invalid and will yield highly biased, unstable metrics.
- **Data Contamination:** The experimental design natively introduces data leakage by evaluating supervised embedding models on their own fine-tuning distributions (SQuAD, NQ, TriviaQA).
- **Vulnerability to Factual Hallucinations:** The fundamental premise assumes cosine similarity equates to factual consistency, ignoring the tendency of dense embeddings to conflate topical similarity with factual equivalence.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To properly evaluate this paper, the entirety of the empirical evaluation is missing. Specifically, the following are required:
- The complete Section 4, including all tables, baseline comparisons (against standard NLI-based Semantic Entropy), and calibration metrics (AUROC, AUARC, ECE).
- A mathematical or empirical proof demonstrating that fitting the vMF parameter $\kappa$ provides a statistically significant advantage over simply using the average pairwise cosine similarity ($\bar{R}$) in rank-based UQ evaluations.
- Ablations on the choice of embedding model, specifically utilizing embedding models that are guaranteed *not* to have seen the evaluation datasets during their contrastive pre-training/fine-tuning phases.
- Anonymized code repositories for reproducibility, detailing the numerical solvers used and the precise hyperparameters for the generative sampling ($T$, top-$p$, $N$).

## What Would Change My Assessment
My assessment would be re-evaluated if the authors provide the complete manuscript featuring comprehensive empirical results that address the benchmark contamination issues. Furthermore, the authors must either (1) mathematically prove that $\kappa^{-1}$ provides utility beyond average cosine similarity in non-monotonic evaluation frameworks, or (2) pivot the paper to explicitly acknowledge that DCU is rank-equivalent to average cosine similarity, framing the vMF derivation as a theoretical justification for a simple baseline rather than a complex novel algorithm. Finally, addressing the $N \ll d$ statistical bias with a corrected high-dimensional estimator would resolve a major theoretical flaw. Without the results section and solutions to the mathematical/statistical flaws, the paper cannot be accepted.

Strong Reject. The provided manuscript is severely incomplete, cutting off before any experimental results are presented, rendering all empirical claims unsupported. Furthermore, the theoretical foundation suffers from statistical invalidity in high dimensions and mathematically reduces to a trivial cosine-similarity baseline, while the intended experimental design suffers from severe benchmark data leakage.
