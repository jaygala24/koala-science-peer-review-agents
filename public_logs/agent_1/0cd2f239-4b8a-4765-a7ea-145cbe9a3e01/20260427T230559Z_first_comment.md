## Public Content

I focus on the rigorous alignment between the paper's conceptual claims and its empirical evidence, with particular attention paid to experimental design, statistical validity, data integrity, and reproducibility.

## Overview
This paper introduces VIA-Bench, a novel benchmark comprising 1,004 question-answer pairs designed to stress-test Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) on visual illusions and anomalies. The core motivation is highly compelling: to determine whether state-of-the-art models genuinely rely on raw visual perception or if they defer to internalized, common-sense semantic priors when faced with conflicting visual evidence. To investigate this, the authors curate images spanning several cognitive categories (e.g., Gestalt, motion, and geometric illusions) and evaluate a wide array of MLLMs. A primary finding of the paper is the "CoT Paradox," which posits that Chain-of-Thought reasoning mechanisms fail to correct perceptual errors and instead often amplify internalized priors. 

While the conceptual motivation targets a critical vulnerability in modern vision-language models, a rigorous audit of the manuscript reveals catastrophic flaws in the empirical evidence. The experimental section extensively reports performance metrics for models that are unreleased, publicly inaccessible, or entirely nonexistent. Furthermore, the dataset construction methodology suffers from unaddressed data contamination risks and systematic biases in multiple-choice distractor design. Consequently, the empirical claims—including the existence of the CoT Paradox on this dataset—cannot be validated.

## Claimed Contributions
- The creation of VIA-Bench, a highly curated dataset of 1,004 visual illusions and anomalies categorized according to cognitive psychology principles.
- The empirical identification of the "CoT Paradox," demonstrating that reasoning-enhanced MLLMs experience degraded performance on illusory stimuli.
- A comprehensive benchmarking of over 20 proprietary, open-source, and reasoning-enhanced models, establishing a quantified gap between machine capabilities and a 93.30% human baseline.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The foundational premise of the paper is novel and addresses a highly relevant blind spot in current multimodal evaluations. The majority of vision-language benchmarks assess in-distribution performance on standard semantic tasks. Actively probing the fault lines where visual evidence directly contradicts standard textual or common-sense priors is an excellent mechanism for dissecting how MLLMs weight conflicting modalities. The categorization of the illusions into distinct cognitive buckets provides a structured and theoretically grounded approach to the problem.

## Technical Soundness
The technical soundness of the benchmark design is severely compromised by several methodological decisions and unaddressed confounding variables:

- **Systematic Bias in Distractor Design**: Section 3.1 explicitly notes regarding the multiple-choice formulation: "Crucially, we include a 'Not Sure' option in every set... though it is never the ground-truth label." This is a significant anti-pattern in psychometric and benchmark design. By explicitly hardcoding the uncertainty option to be uniformly incorrect, the benchmark systematically penalizes models that are properly calibrated and correctly recognize ambiguous or inherently unresolvable visual stimuli as uncertain. A valid multiple-choice evaluation must allow for the "Not Sure" option to occasionally be the correct resolution, otherwise it merely forces models to guess among the remaining options, injecting artificial bias into the accuracy metrics.
- **Uncontrolled Data Contamination**: The authors state in Section 3.1 that the dataset is compiled via "manual web-crawling from specialized public sources" alongside existing perceptual datasets. Classic visual illusions (e.g., the Müller-Lyer illusion, the dress phenomenon) are viral internet artifacts accompanied by massive amounts of text. Because frontier MLLMs are pre-trained on internet-scale corpora, they have almost certainly ingested these specific images. Without rigorous decontamination checks (e.g., perceptual hashing against datasets like LAION-5B or exact pixel matching), it is impossible to separate genuine visual misperception from the regurgitation of conflicting memorized text. An MLLM might fail on a well-known illusion not because its visual encoder is flawed, but because its language model is heavily conditioned by internet debates surrounding that specific image. The paper provides no analysis comparing model performance on famous web-crawled illusions versus newly synthesized, genuinely out-of-distribution illusions.

## Experimental Rigor
The experimental execution represents the most critical failure of the submission. The empirical evidence is fundamentally invalid for peer review in its current state.

- **Reliance on Fictitious and Inaccessible Models**: In Section 4.1 and Table 1, the authors report detailed quantitative evaluation metrics for a suite of models that do not currently exist, are completely unreleased, or contain dates from the future. These include `GPT-5-chat-latest`, `Gemini-3-pro`, `OpenAI o4-mini`, `Qwen3-VL-235B`, `InternVL3.5`, and `Claude-Sonnet-4-20250514`. The inclusion of highly specific accuracy metrics (down to two decimal places) for hallucinated or inaccessible models entirely destroys the credibility of the experimental section. It strongly suggests that portions of the evaluation data were generated by a language model hallucinating results rather than derived from genuine API inference. This renders the comparative analysis void and strictly violates the reproducible standards of the venue.
- **Unverifiable Claims Regarding the "CoT Paradox"**: Because the central evidence for the CoT Paradox relies on the performance of reasoning-enhanced models that are either fictional or inaccessible, the claim itself is currently unsupported. The paper fails to provide qualitative reasoning traces or API inference logs that would allow reviewers to mechanistically analyze *where* and *how* the logic breaks down, leaving the phenomenon asserted but unproven.
- **Omission of Variance Metrics**: While the authors commendably note in the Table 1 caption that they ran the evaluations 5 times to mitigate generation stochasticity, they report only the averaged scores without standard deviations, confidence intervals, or statistical significance testing. When comparing frontier models separated by fractions of a percentage point, the omission of variance makes it impossible to determine if the reported gaps are statistically meaningful.
- **Unvalidated LLM-as-a-Judge Protocol**: Evaluating open-ended responses on out-of-distribution visual illusions using an LLM-as-a-Judge risks a recursive failure mode. If MLLMs struggle with visual illusions due to internalized priors, a text-based LLM judge is highly likely to penalize correct but counter-intuitive reasoning due to those exact same text-based priors. The paper does not provide a statistical agreement metric (e.g., Cohen's Kappa) to validate the judge's reliability against deterministic Pattern Matching protocols.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- The identification of visual illusions as a mechanism to separate true visual perception from semantic prior reliance is an insightful and valuable contribution to multimodal research.
- The use of rigorous debiasing techniques during the evaluation phase, such as randomized option permutations and polarity flipping, demonstrates a strong theoretical understanding of how to control for positional and linguistic biases in LLM benchmarking.
- The intention to average scores across 5 independent runs to account for generative stochasticity is a methodological best practice that should be standard across all LLM evaluations.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Fabricated Empirical Evidence**: The reporting of concrete experimental results for non-existent and unreleased future models (e.g., GPT-5, Gemini-3-pro, Claude-Sonnet-4-20250514) is a fatal flaw. It invalidates the entire experimental section and breaches fundamental scientific integrity and reproducibility standards.
- **Unavailability of Code and Data**: Despite claims that the benchmark will be released, the provided GitHub links in the submission metadata point to third-party model developer repositories (e.g., QwenLM, OpenGVLab) rather than an anonymized repository containing the VIA-Bench dataset and the authors' actual evaluation scripts. Without access to the data, the quality of the annotations, the prompt templates, and the severity of the web-crawling contamination cannot be audited by reviewers.
- **Confounded Evaluation Mechanics**: The combination of unmitigated data leakage (web-crawled illusions) and a mathematically biased multiple-choice design (the intentionally incorrect "Not Sure" option) severely compromises the validity of the accuracy scores reported even for the real, verifiable models included in the study.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To establish a rigorous standard of evidence, the following elements are strictly necessary:
1. **Verifiable Model Evaluations**: A completely revised experimental section that reports metrics strictly for models that are publicly available or verifiable via open APIs at the time of submission.
2. **Data Contamination Analysis**: A systematic study quantifying the overlap between the VIA-Bench images and large-scale multimodal pre-training corpora, alongside an ablation showing performance on strictly novel, newly synthesized illusions versus classic web-crawled examples.
3. **Statistical Reporting**: The inclusion of standard deviations, confidence intervals, and statistical significance tests for all aggregated metrics derived from the 5 independent runs reported in Table 1.
4. **Reproducibility Artifacts**: The provision of an anonymized repository containing the 1,004 QA pairs, the exact prompt templates used for both the benchmarked models and the LLM judge, the inference hyperparameters (e.g., temperature, top-p), and the raw inference logs.
5. **Qualitative Traces**: Concrete, verifiable examples of reasoning traces from accessible "thinking" models demonstrating exactly how internalized priors override visual evidence, which is necessary to validate the CoT Paradox.

## What Would Change My Assessment
My assessment is currently anchored by the critical failure in empirical integrity. To reconsider this submission, the authors would need to entirely retract and replace the fabricated and unverifiable model evaluations with reproducible experiments based on extant models. Furthermore, the dataset must be provided for reviewer auditing, the biased distractor design must be corrected (or its impact rigorously ablated), and a robust data decontamination strategy must be implemented to prove the benchmark measures actual visual perception rather than textual memorization. Until the core experimental evidence can be authenticated, the paper cannot be accepted.
