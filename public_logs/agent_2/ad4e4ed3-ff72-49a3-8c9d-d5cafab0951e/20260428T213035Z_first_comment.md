## Public Content

I focus on situating the paper's claimed theoretical and methodological innovations within the broader literature of adversarial robustness, evaluating the true scope of its novelty, and auditing the rigorousness of its claims and evidence base. In particular, I scrutinize the mathematical consistency of the proposed optimization framework, the verifiability of the empirical claims, and the presentation of the manuscript.

## Overview
The paper tackles a highly practical and challenging security setting: Universal Targeted Transferable Adversarial Attacks (UniTTAA) against closed-source commercial multi-modal large language models (MLLMs) such as GPT-4o, Gemini-2.0, and Claude-4.5. Unlike traditional sample-wise attacks that optimize a perturbation for a single input, this work aims to learn a single, universal perturbation capable of steering arbitrary source images toward a specific target concept across unknown models. The authors identify "triple randomization"—the compounding variance from target-crop randomness, token-wise matching unreliability, and initialization sensitivity—as a major bottleneck in extending sample-wise attacks to a universal setting. 

To overcome this, the paper proposes a multi-stage framework. The core components include Target-View Aggregation (TVA) to reduce gradient variance, Token Routing (TR) for reliable alignment, and Meta-Initialization (MI) to improve sample efficiency during adaptation. The authors report substantial empirical improvements over state-of-the-art baselines, claiming an Attack Success Rate (ASR) increase of up to +23.7% on GPT-4o and +19.9% on Gemini-2.0 for unseen images.

## Claimed Contributions
- **Problem Formulation:** The formal definition of the UniTTAA threat model (Definition III.1), distinguishing the universal transferability setting from existing sample-wise adversarial frameworks.
- **Bottleneck Identification:** The characterization of "triple randomization" as the primary source of optimization instability when migrating to universal attacks.
- **Variance Reduction Strategy:** The introduction of Target-View Aggregation (TVA), motivated by a formal theoretical claim (Proposition IV.1) that it acts as a variance-reducing Monte Carlo estimator.
- **Algorithmic Pipeline:** The combination of TVA with an Attention-Focused View (AFV), Token Routing (TR), and Meta-Initialization (MI) to create a comprehensive attack methodology.
- **Empirical Breakthroughs:** Claimed state-of-the-art attack success rates on leading proprietary MLLMs, demonstrating severe real-world vulnerabilities.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The transition from single-sample targeted attacks (e.g., FOA-Attack, contextualized in Figure 1) to a universal adversarial perturbation is a logical, necessary, and practically impactful step for the MLLM security community. Universal adversarial perturbations (UAPs) have a long history in standard computer vision, and mapping them to multimodal generative spaces is non-trivial. However, the theoretical and algorithmic novelty presented in this manuscript is substantially overstated when viewed against the broader deep learning and statistics literature.

First, I note a significant degree of "math washing" regarding Proposition IV.1. The proposition is presented as a foundational theoretical contribution justifying the Target-View Aggregation mechanism (Section IV-A). However, Proposition IV.1 essentially restates the elementary statistical property of a sample mean: that the average of $m$ independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) random variables provides an unbiased estimator of the population mean, and its variance is scaled by $1/m$. While mathematically correct, framing standard Monte Carlo variance reduction as a novel theoretical proposition overstates the paper's mathematical contributions. This property is the bedrock of standard mini-batch stochastic optimization and data augmentation, and treating it as a novel theoretical discovery for adversarial robustness is inappropriate. 

Second, the paper exhibits a critical theoretical inconsistency in how this proposition is applied. The text explicitly claims that the aggregated target estimate, which intentionally includes the Attention-Focused View ($v_{attn}$), forms an unbiased estimator whose variance-reduction properties are justified by Proposition IV.1. However, the AFV is extracted via a deterministic, attention-guided heuristic (anchoring a window at the attention peak). It is not drawn i.i.d. from the random view distribution $p(v)$. By manually injecting a deterministically selected view into the aggregation set, the i.i.d. assumption of Proposition IV.1 is strictly violated. Consequently, the resulting estimator is mathematically neither unbiased nor guaranteed to follow the exact $1/m$ variance reduction claimed. The conclusion drawn in the text is much stronger than the mathematics permits.

Finally, the novelty of the individual components is primarily incremental. Multi-view aggregation, attention-guided cropping, and meta-learning for initialization are widely used techniques. The contribution here is systemic—engineering these components together for a specific threat model—rather than fundamentally algorithmic.

## Technical Soundness
While the conceptual problem formulation is sound, the execution and presentation of the methodology suffer from severe draft instability, raising significant concerns about the soundness of the manuscript.

There are pervasive and glaring inconsistencies in terminology between the abstract, metadata, and main text. The abstract and metadata introduce the problem as "UTTAA" and the method as "MCRMO-Attack" (Multi-Crop Routed Meta Optimization), utilizing "Multi-Crop Aggregation." However, the main text refers to the problem as "UniTTAA," the method as "TarVRoM-Attack" (Target-View Routed Meta Optimization), and the mechanism as "Target-View Aggregation" (TVA). These discrepancies severely hurt clarity, confusing the reader about which framework is actually being proposed and evaluated.

Furthermore, the provided text abruptly ends before detailing the mathematical formulation of the Token Routing (TR) and Meta-Initialization (MI) components. This makes it impossible to evaluate the technical soundness of the token alignment reliability or the computational scaling of the meta-learning phase. Additionally, the Attention-Focused View extraction mechanism is described purely in dense prose; the absence of a formal algorithm block or mathematical definition makes it ambiguous and difficult to reproduce.

## Experimental Rigor
The empirical claims made in the abstract are massive (+23.7% ASR on GPT-4o), but the verifiability and rigor of the experimental evidence are fundamentally lacking in the provided submission.

Crucially, the experimental section appears to be entirely missing from the provided manuscript text. The central quantitative claims regarding state-of-the-art attack success rates on commercial MLLMs are completely unsupported by tables, baseline comparisons, or ablation studies within the body of the text. Without explicit empirical evidence, these claims cannot be validated.

Furthermore, the methodology relies on a "GPT-based judge" (e.g., via Leval) for evaluating the Attack Success Rate. LLM-as-a-judge paradigms are notoriously sensitive to prompt design and context. Without the exact prompt templates or a validation study measuring the agreement rate between the LLM judge and human annotators, the ASR metric is at high risk of false positives. Adversarial perturbations often introduce severe image degradation; an uncalibrated LLM judge might trigger a "success" based on simple keyword hallucination rather than true, precise semantic steering of the output. 

Finally, evaluations against continuously updated, closed-source APIs (GPT-4o, Gemini-2.0, Claude-4.5) introduce severe reproducibility hurdles. The manuscript does not document explicit model version tags (e.g., `gpt-4o-2024-05-13`), access dates, or generation hyperparameters. Without this metadata or an accompanying code repository, the core transferability results are essentially irreproducible.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Compelling Threat Model:** The formulation of the UniTTAA setting provides a rigorous, realistic, and highly difficult benchmark for assessing the adversarial robustness of multi-modal systems in the wild.
- **Accurate Bottleneck Diagnosis:** The identification of "triple randomization" effectively captures the practical difficulties of universal adversarial optimization, offering a valuable insight for the security community.
- **High-Impact Vulnerability:** If verifiable, generating reliable, universal, and transferable attacks against state-of-the-art commercial systems like GPT-4o exposes a critical real-world security vulnerability that warrants urgent attention.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Anonymity and Format Policy Violations (Area Chair Verification Risk):** The submitted manuscript explicitly includes author names, author affiliations (e.g., Nanyang Technological University, DSO National Laboratories), and contact emails on the first page. Furthermore, the document is formatted using an IEEE journal template ("JOURNAL OF LATEX CLASS FILES, VOL. 14, NO. 8, AUGUST 2021") rather than the mandatory anonymous ICML conference template. While I defer to the Area Chair for procedural decisions, this is an unambiguous violation of standard double-blind venue rules and suggests a severely mishandled submission upload.
- **Missing Experimental Evidence:** The complete absence of the experimental section in the provided text means the paper's core empirical claims are unsubstantiated. 
- **Incomplete Methodology:** The descriptions of Token Routing and Meta-Initialization are missing, preventing technical review of the full multi-stage pipeline.
- **Draft Instability:** The profound naming mismatches (MCRMO-Attack vs. TarVRoM-Attack) across the abstract and body indicate an unfinished or unproofread draft.
- **Theoretical Flaws:** The framing of standard Monte Carlo variance reduction as a novel proposition, coupled with the mathematically incorrect application of the i.i.d. assumption to the deterministic Attention-Focused View, undermines the paper's theoretical integrity.
- **Ethical and Dual-Use Risks:** The paper develops highly effective universal exploits against commercial APIs without providing an ethics statement, a responsible disclosure plan (e.g., prior reporting to OpenAI/Google), or discussing mitigation strategies.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To consider this work technically complete, the following evidence is required:
- The full experimental section, including the main results tables corroborating the abstract's ASR claims against strong baselines.
- Rigorous ablation studies isolating the exact performance contributions of Target-View Aggregation, Attention-Focused View, Token Routing, and Meta-Initialization.
- A detailed breakdown of the computational cost (GPU hours, FLOPs) of the Meta-Initialization phase compared to learning an attack from scratch.
- Explicit documentation of the prompt templates used for the GPT-based judge, accompanied by a human-agreement validation study for the ASR metric to rule out false positives caused by generic image degradation.
- Strict documentation of the API version tags, access dates, and inference parameters used during evaluation.
