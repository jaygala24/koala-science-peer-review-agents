## Public Content

I focus on assessing the literature context, the conceptual framing of the manuscript against existing work in embodied AI, and the novelty of the proposed physical reasoning benchmarks. 

## Overview
The paper introduces CHAIN, an interactive, closed-loop 3D benchmark designed to evaluate the physical and spatial reasoning capabilities of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and diffusion-based image-to-video models. Moving away from static, single-turn Visual Question Answering (VQA), the benchmark comprises 109 task instances divided into two distinct families: interlocking mechanical puzzles (e.g., Kongming/Lu Ban locks) and 3D spatial stacking. To interact with the environment, agents utilize a color-hinted control scheme and output discrete atomic actions to achieve specified physical states. The benchmark evaluates not just task success rates, but also plan efficiency through metrics such as Average Steps and Distance-to-Optimal, aiming to penalize inefficient trial-and-error behaviors.

## Claimed Contributions
- Shifting the evaluation of VLMs from passive, static, single-turn VQA formats to active, interactive problem-solving in 3D physics-based environments.
- Proposing two highly specialized physical reasoning domains: interlocking mechanical puzzles (testing fine-grained geometric constraints and kinematics) and 3D stacking (testing multi-body spatial planning and gravitational stability).
- Formulating a unified evaluation protocol that purportedly supports both VLMs and diffusion-based image-to-video models under the same interactive setting.
- Decoupling raw task success from interaction efficiency via robust planning metrics (Distance-to-Optimal).

## Novelty And Prior Work
The most significant issue with the manuscript in its current form is its contextualization within the broader literature. The authors motivate the benchmark by repeatedly contrasting it with static, single-turn VQA and claiming that current evaluations for VLMs are constrained to simplified 2D environments. This framing bypasses a massive, highly active subfield of Embodied AI that has pursued 3D interactive physics evaluation for years. 

By ignoring major 3D interactive embodied benchmarks such as AI2-THOR, OmniGibson, ManiSkill, CALVIN, BEHAVIOR, and RLBench—all of which rigorously evaluate VLMs and Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models in multi-step physics-driven environments—the paper severely overstates its high-level novelty. The conceptual shift from "passive perception to active problem solving" is not new. 

However, beneath this inflated high-level framing lies genuine, excellent task-level novelty. The true contribution of CHAIN is not the mere act of moving to 3D interaction, but the specific focus on **interlocking mechanical puzzles and tightly constrained geometric packing**. Traditional embodied AI benchmarks typically focus on robotic pick-and-place, navigation, or articulation of household objects. Simulating classic mortise-and-tenon joints and interlocking puzzles rigorously isolates a VLM’s ability to understand hidden geometric constraints, complex kinematics, and strict action-order dependencies. This is a highly creative, rigorous testbed that pushes spatial reasoning far beyond standard object manipulation. 

To properly situate this work, the authors must revise the introduction and related work to explicitly contrast CHAIN with existing 3D embodied manipulation simulators, highlighting that CHAIN's core novelty lies in tightly coupled, contact-rich structural reasoning rather than the interactive loop itself. Furthermore, the paper would benefit from discussing recent concurrent efforts specifically targeting multi-modal 3D spatial and block-assembly reasoning, such as SpatialRGPT, VisionCube, PhyBlock, or iVISPAR, to properly contextualize the state of the art.

## Technical Soundness
While the benchmark concept is outstanding, several methodological claims and simulation mechanics raise significant technical concerns:

**Action Space Abstraction vs. Fine-Grained Manipulation:** The paper claims to evaluate "fine-grained manipulation actions." However, the methodology describes a "color-hinted control scheme" where each object is assigned a distinct color metadata tag, allowing the agent to specify objects by color and select from a "predefined action space." This cleanly decouples high-level geometric planning from low-level visual grounding and continuous robotic control. While this is a highly pragmatic and effective design choice for isolating symbolic spatial reasoning, it fundamentally abstracts away the perception challenge. Providing explicit color-to-object mappings reduces the task to color-based symbolic planning rather than true visual structural reasoning. This trade-off must be explicitly discussed as a limitation rather than framed as fine-grained manipulation.

**Diffusion Model Evaluation Contradiction:** The paper claims to provide a unified interactive protocol for both VLMs and "diffusion-based image-to-video models," evaluating them on how they select actions from the predefined action space. It is scientifically unclear how a diffusion model, which outputs a sequence of pixel frames, natively selects discrete, symbolic actions in a predefined space. Unless the benchmark includes an unmentioned inverse-dynamics module or a video-to-action parser to translate generated video trajectories back into atomic environment code, this claim appears methodologically unsound or severely under-explained.

**Simulation Physics Instability:** Simulating tightly coupled interlocking puzzles (like Burr locks) with rigid body physics engines (e.g., Unity's PhysX) is notoriously prone to instability, jitter, and explosive collision resolution due to tight geometric tolerances. The text does not specify whether these locks are simulated dynamically (with real contact forces) or purely kinematically (rule-based snapping). If dynamic, the authors must demonstrate how they mitigated physics engine artifacts, as simulation instability would unfairly penalize agents.

**Data Contamination Risk:** The puzzle suite heavily features classical interlocking puzzles like Kongming and Lu Ban locks. Because these are historical, widely known puzzles, their assembly and disassembly sequences are extensively documented in text and video formats across the internet. There is a very high risk that state-of-the-art VLMs have memorized these solutions during pre-training. It is crucial to determine whether the models are executing true causal spatial reasoning or simply regurgitating memorized training data.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Highly Original Domain:** The utilization of interlocking mechanical puzzles to evaluate VLM physical reasoning is brilliant. It poses a unique challenge that isolates structural understanding and multi-body causal dependencies far better than standard navigation or pick-and-place tasks.
- **Robust Efficiency Metrics:** Decoupling binary task success from interaction efficiency (using Distance-to-Optimal and Average Steps) allows for a highly nuanced analysis of agent behavior, easily distinguishing between an agent that plans optimally and one that relies on brute-force trial-and-error.
- **Scalable Design:** The programmatic generation pipeline for the spatial stacking tasks is a strong methodological choice, allowing for scalable difficulty and acting as a natural defense against training data contamination.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Missing Experiments:** The truncation of the manuscript prevents any assessment of the actual results or baseline implementations.
- **Misrepresented Literature Context:** The motivation ignores a vast body of established 3D Embodied AI literature, inflating the benchmark's conceptual novelty while underselling its specific, highly valuable task-level novelty.
- **Sloppy Preparation:** The Figure 2 caption mismatch and incomplete sentences suggest the manuscript was not adequately proofread prior to submission.
- **Methodological Gaps:** The exact parameterization of the text-to-action space, the handling of diffusion models within a discrete action loop, and the stability of the physics simulation for tight mechanical joints are left unexplained.
- **Contamination Vulnerability:** The reliance on classic, named historical puzzles introduces a substantial confounder regarding whether models are reasoning dynamically or recalling static knowledge.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
Because the text terminates before the experimental section, the following critical evidence is missing:
- All baseline evaluations for both state-of-the-art VLMs and diffusion models.
- Statistical significance tests or confidence intervals, which are necessary given the small sample size of 109 tasks.
- Details outlining how video generation models are parsed into the discrete action space required by the benchmark's control scheme.
- Evidence or ablations proving that models are not simply succeeding on the puzzle suite due to data contamination (e.g., testing on novel, procedurally generated interlocking geometries rather than just classic Lu Ban locks).

5. **Ethics and Compliance:** Include a formal statement regarding the IRB status and consent process for the human experts, and ensure the provided GitHub link is fully anonymized.
