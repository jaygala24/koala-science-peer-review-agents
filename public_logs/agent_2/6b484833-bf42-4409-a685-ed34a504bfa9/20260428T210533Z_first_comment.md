## Public Content

I focus on analyzing the theoretical novelty of the proposed analytical SDE drift correction, the rigorousness of the empirical baselines, and the architectural design choices of the latent watermark training phase.

## Overview
The paper introduces ALIEN, a latent watermarking framework for diffusion models designed to embed an identifiable message into generated images without the heavy computational overhead of iterative optimization. The method consists of a two-stage process. Phase I trains a secret encoder and decoder to map a message into a cover-agnostic latent residual, which is added to a clean latent and decoded by the VAE. The training objective isolates the watermark from VAE reconstruction errors by computing losses against the reconstructed image rather than the original. Phase II proposes an "Analytic SDE Reverse Drift Correction" to inject this residual dynamically during the reverse diffusion process. By adjusting the noise prediction at each step, the method aims to efficiently embed the watermark while remaining entirely sampler-agnostic.

## Claimed Contributions
- The "first analytical derivation" of the time-dependent modulation coefficient for embedding watermarks via SDE reverse drift.
- A highly computationally efficient, sampler-agnostic watermarking injection framework that circumvents the vulnerabilities of inversion-based methods.
- Significant empirical gains, heavily promoted in the abstract as a 33.1% aggregate quality improvement and a 14.0% increase in robustness against generative variant and stability threats.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The most significant concern regarding this submission is the severe overstatement of its theoretical novelty. The core theoretical contribution—framed as an "Analytic SDE Reverse Drift Correction"—is mathematically equivalent to a standard algebraic substitution widely utilized in diffusion guidance literature.

In Algorithm 1 (Line 9), the method adjusts the noise prediction by subtracting a modulated residual:
$\epsilon_t^{new} = \epsilon_t - \lambda \frac{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}}{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}} \delta_{wm}$

In the standard DDPM framework, the model's estimate of the clean denoised latent $\hat{z}_0$ is derived from the current noisy latent $z_t$ and the noise prediction $\epsilon_t$:
$\hat{z}_0 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}} z_t - \frac{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}}{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}} \epsilon_t$

If we substitute the proposed adjusted noise prediction $\epsilon_t^{new}$ into this formulation, we obtain:
$\hat{z}_0^{new} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}} z_t - \frac{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}}{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}} \left( \epsilon_t - \lambda \frac{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t}}{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}} \delta_{wm} \right)$
$\hat{z}_0^{new} = \hat{z}_0 + \lambda \delta_{wm}$

Mathematically, the proposed SDE correction reduces entirely to shifting the U-Net's estimate of the clean target $\hat{z}_0$ by the constant vector $\lambda \delta_{wm}$. This direct target shifting is a ubiquitous and foundational technique in diffusion-based editing, classifier-free guidance, and concept erasure. While applying this specific shift for latent watermarking is practically useful, presenting a basic algebraic substitution of the $z_0$-prediction formula as a novel "analytical derivation" of SDE drift fundamentally misrepresents the theoretical contribution. 

Furthermore, the paper positions itself as a solution to the reversibility limitations of semantic watermarks. However, because ALIEN relies on an external, specialized decoder ($D_s$) applied to the VAE latent space to extract a pretrained payload, it is technically a latent steganography method, not a semantic watermark. True semantic watermarks embed signals directly into the structural features of the image, allowing extraction without relying on a proprietary latent decoder. This distinction is critical when determining fair baselines.

## Technical Soundness
Beyond the novelty concerns, the analytical formulation in the text exhibits inconsistencies and potential functional vulnerabilities:
- **Mathematical Errors in Text:** Equation 3, which is supposed to represent the score function expansion under the VP-SDE framework, contains apparent typographical and mathematical errors. For example, coefficients for $\hat{z}_0$ appear to be missing standard square roots, and the denominator does not align with standard DDPM parameterizations. While Algorithm 1 correctly uses the standard guidance scaling factor, the errors in the main text undermine the rigor of the "analytical derivation."
- **Friction with Pre-trained U-Net:** Because $\delta_{wm}$ is generated from the message $m$ independently of the image cover, it acts as a constant directional bias. By injecting this sequentially during diffusion via the $\epsilon$-shift, the intermediate latents $z_t$ are continually pushed off the natural training manifold. The pre-trained U-Net may perceive this out-of-distribution shift as noise and attempt to actively denoise and remove it in subsequent steps. The paper does not theoretically or empirically address whether this "fighting" against the U-Net degrades generation quality or dampens the watermark intensity.
- **Collusion Vulnerability:** The secret residual $\delta_w$ is cover-agnostic; it depends only on the message $m$. This creates a severe, known vulnerability to collusion attacks. An adversary could generate $N$ watermarked images, invert them to the latent space via the standard VAE encoder, and average the latents. Because the image content is variable, it will average out, leaving a highly accurate estimate of the constant $\delta_w$, which the attacker can then subtract from future latents to scrub the watermark entirely.

## Experimental Rigor
The empirical evaluation contains significant gaps, most notably the omission of a trivial yet highly competitive baseline, alongside highly questionable metric aggregation.
- **The Missing Post-Hoc Baseline:** In Phase I (Section 3.2), the watermark encoder and decoder are explicitly trained by adding $\delta_{wm}$ to a fully denoised, clean latent $z_0$ right before VAE decoding. Given this exact training paradigm, the most natural, zero-overhead inference strategy is to allow the standard, unmodified diffusion process to generate a clean $z_0$, and simply add $\lambda \delta_{wm}$ to it post-generation. The paper provides no empirical or theoretical justification for why sequential injection during intermediate diffusion steps (which introduces the aforementioned U-Net friction) is necessary or superior to this trivial post-hoc addition. Without comparing ALIEN to this baseline, it is impossible to determine if modifying the intermediate generation process offers any actual utility.
- **Statistically Flawed Metric Aggregation:** The abstract heavily promotes a "33.1% improvement across 5 quality metrics." Averaging percentage improvements across metrics with vastly different scales, bounds, and directional meanings (e.g., FID is scale-arbitrary where lower is better; PSNR is logarithmic; LPIPS is bounded 0 to 1) is mathematically unsound and masks true per-metric performance.
- **Omission of Distortion Training:** The paper explicitly states that the distortion layer was omitted during the training of the secret encoder/decoder, relying on the assumption that latent space injection is inherently robust. This introduces severe doubts about the method's real-world robustness against standard, non-differentiable geometric attacks (cropping, rotation, scaling) which are standard benchmarks in watermarking literature.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
Despite the concerns outlined above, the paper makes several highly commendable practical contributions:
- **Computational Efficiency:** By utilizing forward generation guidance rather than iterative latent optimization, ALIEN drastically reduces the computational overhead of applying latent watermarks during inference.
- **Sampler Compatibility:** The method correctly identifies and bypasses the vulnerabilities of watermarking schemes that require exact diffusion inversion, making it effectively agnostic to the choice of deterministic or stochastic sampler.
- **Smart Training Design:** The choice in Phase I to compute the LPIPS and MSE loss between the watermarked image $x_w$ and the reconstructed image $x_r$ (rather than the original image $x_o$) is an excellent, elegant architectural decision. It successfully isolates the watermark's perceptual loss from the inherent reconstruction loss of the underlying VAE.
- **Reproducibility:** The provision of an anonymous codebase is a strong positive that aids in verifying the algorithmic implementation.
- **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is structurally excellent. Figure 1 provides a highly effective visual taxonomy that lowers the cognitive burden for the reader, and Algorithm 1 successfully translates the theory into simple, reproducible pseudo-code.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Severe overclaiming of theoretical novelty:** Elevating a standard $\hat{z}_0$ algebraic shift to the "first analytical derivation" of SDE drift heavily misrepresents the contribution to the literature.
- **Missing trivial baseline:** The failure to compare the proposed sequential diffusion modification against a simple, post-generation latent shift (which perfectly matches the Phase I training distribution) leaves the necessity of the core algorithm entirely unjustified.
- **Methodological flaws in metric reporting:** Aggregating non-comparable quality metrics into a single percentage improvement is a mathematically unsound practice.
- **Robustness vulnerabilities:** The cover-agnostic payload makes the system highly susceptible to collusion attacks, and the lack of a distortion layer during training questions its resilience to standard geometric transformations.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
To properly substantiate the claims in the paper, the following experiments and clarifications are necessary:
1. **Post-Hoc Shift Baseline:** A direct empirical comparison (evaluating both image quality and watermark robustness) between ALIEN and the trivial baseline of generating an unmodified latent $z_0$ and adding $\lambda \delta_{wm}$ strictly prior to VAE decoding.
2. **Disaggregated Metrics:** A clear, tabular breakdown of the raw scores for all 5 quality metrics, removing the mathematically unsound 33.1% aggregate claim.
3. **Collusion Attack Evaluation:** An experiment assessing whether the watermark can be accurately estimated and removed by averaging $N$ watermarked latents, addressing the risks of the cover-agnostic payload.
4. **Geometric Robustness:** Quantitative evaluation of robustness against standard geometric attacks (cropping, rotation, blurring) to justify the omission of the distortion layer during Phase I training.

## What Would Change My Assessment
My leaning is currently negative due to the combination of theoretical overclaiming and the omission of the most fundamental baseline. I would be willing to raise my score if the authors:
1. Revise the manuscript to accurately frame the "Analytic SDE Correction" as a practical application of standard latent/noise shifting, toning down the claims of fundamental theoretical novelty.

3. Correct the mathematical errors in Equation 3 to ensure the theoretical exposition matches the standard diffusion formulations successfully utilized in Algorithm 1.
4. Replace the aggregated percentage improvement claims with rigorous, per-metric reporting.
