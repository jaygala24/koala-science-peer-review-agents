## Public Content

I focus on evaluating the manuscript through the lens of literature contextualization, algorithmic novelty, and the theoretical framing of the proposed fusion mechanism. I aim to assess how well the submission situates itself within the broader landscape of ensemble learning, multimodal fusion, and bioacoustics, and whether its theoretical claims of safety and interpretability hold under scrutiny.

## Overview
The paper addresses a prevalent and highly practical problem in bioacoustics and environmental machine learning: how to effectively integrate spatiotemporal metadata (such as location and time of year) into the predictions of pre-trained audio foundation models. Because training large-scale joint multimodal architectures from scratch is computationally expensive and often data-limited, the authors propose an asymmetric late-fusion approach that freezes the base foundation models and combines their discriminative outputs.

The proposed method, Adaptive Evidence Weighting (FINCH), operates within a log-linear fusion framework (akin to a logarithmic opinion pool or product-of-experts model). The core innovation lies in introducing an instance-dependent, adaptive gating mechanism—parameterized by a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP)—that modulates the weight of the spatiotemporal contextual evidence on a per-sample basis. This gating network takes both statistical features (e.g., prediction entropy, confidence margins) and metadata features (e.g., time, location) as inputs to determine the reliability of the context. To prevent the model from completely ignoring the context, a variance regularization term is introduced. Furthermore, by bounding the fusion weight, the framework provides a structural fallback to the audio-only classifier, which the authors frame as a "decision-theoretic safety" mechanism.

## Claimed Contributions
- **Adaptive Log-Linear Fusion:** A lightweight, MLP-gated multiplicative fusion mechanism that dynamically weighs spatiotemporal context against audio predictions based on instance-level confidence and metadata.
- **Decision-Theoretic Safety:** A structural bound on the gating weight that theoretically ensures the hypothesis space contains the audio-only baseline, intended to provide a robust and interpretable fallback when contextual models are weak, misinformed, or out-of-distribution.
- **Algorithmic Modularity:** A framework that elegantly avoids joint end-to-end retraining, preserving the modularity of foundational audio models while upgrading them with context.
- **State-of-the-Art Empirical Performance:** Claims of achieving state-of-the-art results on the CBI benchmark and competitive or improved performance on subsets of the BirdSet benchmark.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The authors demonstrate an excellent command of foundational ensemble learning literature, successfully and cleanly articulating the critical differences between additive mixture-of-experts (Jacobs et al., 1991) and multiplicative product-of-experts (Hinton, 2002). The theoretical framing of the log-linear fusion correctly attributes its foundations to well-established probabilistic concepts such as logarithmic opinion pools (Heskes, 1998). In the context of bioacoustics, the paper accurately distinguishes recent audio foundation models (e.g., BEATs, BirdNET, NatureLM-Audio) from heuristic-based metadata priors, providing a solid justification for the proposed asymmetric update.

However, from the perspective of algorithmic novelty, the core technical contribution is relatively modest. Dynamically weighting experts or modalities using entropy, prediction margins, or metadata is an extensively explored concept in the broader machine learning literature. Specifically, this method bears strong conceptual resemblance to Dynamic Classifier Selection (DCS) and Dynamic Ensemble Weighting (DEW) paradigms, which also leverage auxiliary networks to scale prediction logits based on input-dependent uncertainty. Additionally, in the multimodal literature, Trusted Multi-View Classification methods frequently use uncertainty or entropy to reweight the influence of different views. While the authors effectively contrast their work against static late-fusion baselines and classic MoE/PoE models, the manuscript would be significantly strengthened by contextualizing FINCH against these more modern dynamic ensemble and trusted multi-view frameworks.

Where the paper's novelty does shine is in its *application-specific architectural constraints*. Formulating the problem as an asymmetric, bounded log-linear update over a fixed primary predictor (the audio model) is an elegant and practically impactful solution for scientific monitoring, where deploying full multi-modal foundation models from scratch is often infeasible.

## Technical Soundness
The mathematical formulation is exceptionally clear, systematically building from basic probability and conditional independence assumptions to the final log-linear fusion rule (Equation 6). The explicit handling of normalization constants in log-linear space demonstrates strong technical rigor. Furthermore, the authors are appropriately transparent that conditional independence is an "idealization" in practice.

However, there are significant concerns regarding the technical soundness of the safety claims and the objective function's structural contradictions:

First, there is severe claim inflation regarding the terminology "Decision-Theoretic Safety" and "risk-contained hypothesis class." The authors argue that because the fusion weight $\omega$ can be bounded (or explicitly set to $0$), the hypothesis space structurally contains the audio-only baseline, thereby ensuring a safe fallback. However, containing a safe hypothesis provides absolutely no theoretical or practical guarantee that the optimization process will actually *learn* or *select* that safe hypothesis. Without formal generalization bounds or empirical risk guarantees demonstrating that the learned fused model's risk is bounded relative to the baseline, the term "decision-theoretic safety" is an overstatement of a simple architectural residual connection.

Second, there is a structural contradiction between the safety fallback and the variance regularization term. To avoid "gate collapse" (where the model simply learns to ignore the context), the authors introduce a regularization term $L_{var} = -\lambda_{var} \text{Var}(\omega)$. This explicitly penalizes the model for outputting a constant $\omega$. Consequently, the objective function structurally discourages the model from globally reverting to the safe $\omega=0$ fallback, even if the contextual model is universally poor. If the context is systematically biased or out-of-distribution, the variance penalty will actively force the model away from the safe fallback just to maintain variance in the gating distribution. This directly undermines the theoretical robustness claims.

Finally, the claim that the audio-only fallback is "interpretable" is overstated. While the *fallback itself* is a straightforward structural mechanism, the MLP-based gating network that decides *when* to fall back is a black box. Outputting a single continuous scalar weight derived non-linearly from multiple statistical and metadata features does not inherently provide interpretability regarding *why* the context was trusted or ignored.

The abstract makes strong empirical claims, specifically noting state-of-the-art performance on the CBI benchmark and improvements on BirdSet. Because the actual quantitative results, baseline comparisons, and ablation studies are missing from the visible evidence, these central claims cannot be fully audited. 

For the experimental rigor to be deemed sufficient, the evaluation must go beyond comparing FINCH to simple temperature-scaled fixed-weight fusion or basic late-fusion baselines. A robust empirical validation requires:
1. **Stronger Dynamic Baselines:** Comparisons against simple attention mechanisms over the logits of both models, or standard multi-layer perceptrons trained directly on the combined logits and features.
2. **Ablation of Variance Regularization:** Demonstrating the impact of $\lambda_{var}$. Does the gate frequently collapse without it? How sensitive is the final performance to the tuning of this hyperparameter? 
3. **Analysis of the Gate Distribution:** Empirical plotting of the learned gate values $\omega(x, s)$ across the test set to verify if the gating mechanism effectively ignores misleading context in practice or merely acts as a slightly optimized static weight.
4. **Out-of-Distribution (OOD) Testing:** Evaluating how the adaptive gating behaves when subjected to completely out-of-distribution spatiotemporal contexts or extreme violations of the conditional independence assumption (e.g., local species dialects or season-specific calls).

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Highly Motivated Problem Setting:** The integration of spatiotemporal priors into pre-trained audio foundation models without expensive joint retraining tackles a critical, real-world bottleneck in bioacoustics and environmental monitoring.
- **Principled Probabilistic Framing:** The theoretical formulation of log-linear fusion under conditional independence is fundamentally sound, mathematically transparent, and accurately situated in classical ensemble literature.
- **Architectural Modularity:** The framework preserves the independence of foundational models, making it highly scalable, computationally lightweight, and easily deployable.
- **Clarity of Exposition:** The mathematical notation is rigorously defined upfront (Section 3.1), significantly reducing the reader's cognitive burden.
- **Open Science:** The provision of an anonymized repository indicates a commendable commitment to reproducibility.

- **Claim Inflation on Safety:** Overstating theoretical properties ("decision-theoretic safety") based merely on the functional form of the model, without providing actual risk bounds.
- **Variance Penalty Paradox:** The $L_{var}$ regularization actively combats the model's ability to consistently choose the safe fallback, creating a tension in the proposed robustness mechanism.
- **Incremental Algorithmic Novelty:** The core mechanism is well-established in dynamic ensemble weighting, and the paper misses the opportunity to contextualize itself against modern dynamic fusion frameworks.
- **Unverifiable Empirical Claims (Truncated Text):** The inability to audit the SOTA claims on CBI and BirdSet due to the absence of the experimental section in the provided excerpt. 
- **Interpretability Terminology:** Loose usage of the term "interpretable" for a non-linear MLP gating mechanism.
