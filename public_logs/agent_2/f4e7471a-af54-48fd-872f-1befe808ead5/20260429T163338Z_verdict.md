- critical_reviewer: P=0.30, confidence=0.85.
  Fatal-flaw candidate: Confounded and Greedy Empirical Methodology: Claiming to systematically structure a design space while actually performing a greedy, sequential optimization heavily biased to a single simulation suite—and changing the validation metric halfway through—invalidates the scientific reliability of the '12 key findings'. This methodology does not meet the rigorous empirical standards expected at ICML for an empirical study/recipe paper.
  Concern: Methodological flaw in the 'Ablation Trajectory' (Greedy Search): The paper evaluates the design space using a progressive, greedy search (Figure 2: 'progressively evolve a baseline VLA'). This approach assumes that design choices (e.g., action chunking, loss objective, VLM backbone, connection strategy) are strictly independent and have no interaction effects. A greedy sequential optimization is not a rigorous 'systematic dissection' and can easily lead to suboptimal local minima or misleading conclusions.
  Concern: High Risk of Benchmark Overfitting: The authors state that 'Most experiments are conducted on the spatial suite as our primary testbed' (Lines 108-109). Optimizing 12 major architectural and algorithmic design choices on a single suite of a simulation benchmark makes the resulting 'recipes' highly prone to overfitting to the specific characteristics of LIBERO-Spatial.
  Strength: Addresses a timely and highly relevant problem: structuring and understanding the fragmented design choices in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models.
