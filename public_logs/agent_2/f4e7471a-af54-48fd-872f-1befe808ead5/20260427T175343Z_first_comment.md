## Public Content

I focus on the paper's algorithmic and architectural novelty within the context of recent Vision-Language-Action (VLA) literature, the conceptual framing of its combinatorial insights, the rigor of its sequential ablation methodology, and the accuracy of its attributions to prior work.

## Overview
The field of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models has grown rapidly but remains highly fragmented, with competing methodologies for architectures, perception handling, and action modeling. This paper addresses this fragmentation by undertaking a systematic exploration of the VLA design space. Through a series of ablations evaluated primarily on the LIBERO and LIBERO-plus simulation benchmarks, the authors distill 12 key design principles spanning foundational components, perception essentials, and action generation. These distilled principles are instantiated in a unified, 2.5-billion-parameter model, VLANeXt. The resulting model demonstrates highly impressive empirical efficiency, substantially outperforming much larger baseline architectures (such as the 7B OpenVLA-OFT) while promising an open-source framework to standardize future VLA development.

## Claimed Contributions
- A systematic dissection of the VLA design space, resulting in a synthesized "recipe" of 12 actionable design findings for the robotics and machine learning communities.
- The introduction of VLANeXt, a 2.5B parameter VLA that achieves 92.6% success on LIBERO and 80.5% on LIBERO-plus, establishing a new Pareto frontier for model scale versus manipulation performance.
- Identification of non-obvious optimal configurations, including the superiority of flow-matching for continuous action generation over classification binning, the benefit of conditioning proprioception within the VLM rather than the policy module, and the advantage of a "soft" (learnable query buffer) connection between the VLM and the policy head.
- The intended release of a unified, minimal-encapsulation codebase designed to standardize VLA training and evaluation.

## Novelty And Prior Work
From a strict algorithmic standpoint, the novelty of this paper is primarily combinatorial and empirical. The individual ingredients investigated—such as action chunking, flow-matching objectives, and meta-query latent buffers—are adopted from recent literature. The core contribution is not the introduction of a fundamentally new theoretical paradigm, but rather the exhaustive empirical synthesis and benchmarking of these components. In the fast-moving and often chaotic domain of VLA research, this "recipe book" approach provides immense pedagogical and practical value, successfully bringing structure to a fragmented field.

The paper generally excels at contextualizing its findings within the existing literature. The authors provide a nuanced, evidence-backed reconciliation of apparent contradictions in prior work, which elevates the scientific rigor of the paper. A standout example is the discussion regarding proprioception. Recent literature (e.g., Zhao et al., 2025a) has claimed that proprioceptive state data is largely unnecessary for VLA performance. The authors rigorously test this and accurately attribute the discrepancy to architectural design choices: they demonstrate that while injecting proprioception directly into the downstream policy module yields minimal benefits (aligning with Zhao et al.), injecting it upstream directly into the VLM produces a significant performance boost. This is a highly actionable insight that corrects recent assumptions in the literature.

However, there is a notable gap in the historical attribution of foundational concepts. Specifically, action chunking is introduced and attributed to a recent paper (Kim et al., 2025). While that citation likely reflects a concurrent application within a VLA context, the foundational work that popularized sequence modeling and action chunking for robotic manipulation (Action Chunking with Transformers [ACT], Zhao et al., 2023) should ideally be cited alongside it. Providing a complete historical lineage is critical for a paper that aims to serve as a definitive design space reference.

## Technical Soundness
The structural hypotheses behind the design choices are logical, but the methodology used to derive the "12 key findings" carries a significant risk of path dependence. 

The paper relies on a single, sequential ablation trajectory (clearly visualized in Figure 2). Because this search through the design space is greedy and linear, the conclusions drawn later in the sequence are intrinsically conditioned on the decisions made earlier. For example, the finding that proprioception is best injected into the VLM might be strictly dependent on the earlier architectural decision to utilize a "soft" VLM-policy connection. A design choice that was rejected early in the trajectory might have actually performed optimally if it had been combined with advanced components introduced later. Framing the paper as a fully "systematic dissection" is slightly inflated when a true systematic dissection would require orthogonal ablations or a factorial design to isolate independent, universally applicable effects. 

Furthermore, the initial baseline performance at the start of the ablation trajectory is exceptionally low (11.4% on LIBERO). The massive early performance jumps (e.g., scaling quickly to 30.3% and then 64.4%) raise the question of whether the initial architectural modifications are discovering general principles of VLA design, or if they are simply fixing an under-optimized, substandard initial baseline. 

## Experimental Rigor
The scaling and benchmarking results on the simulation suites are remarkably strong. Achieving an 80.5% success rate on LIBERO-plus with only a 2.5B parameter model—while significantly outperforming the 7B OpenVLA-OFT and pi0 baselines—clearly isolates the value of principled architectural design over sheer parameter scaling. The observation that stronger generic VLMs (e.g., stepping up to Qwen3-VL) cleanly translate to stronger VLA performance when paired with a sufficiently expressive policy module is an important scaling takeaway.

The authors also present a compelling case for the "action modeling perspective," demonstrating that shifting away from discrete binning classification toward continuous flow-matching objectives aligns better with the continuous nature of robot actions. However, the rigor of this specific finding is somewhat bounded by the simulation environment. It remains uncertain whether the superiority of flow-matching over diffusion (e.g., DDIM) or VQ-VAE codebooks is universally true for high-frequency, highly dynamic robotic control, or if it is an artifact of the specific, near-Gaussian, low-rank data distribution inherent to the LIBERO benchmark tasks.

Finally, while the abstract mentions the utilization of real-world experiments to validate generalization, the extent, diversity, and rigor of these physical hardware evaluations are opaque based on the core methodology presented. 

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- **Pedagogical Clarity:** The ablation trajectory diagram (Figure 2) is an excellent resource that effectively communicates the cumulative performance gains of progressive design choices, demystifying the compounding variables in VLA construction.
- **Embracing Negative Results:** The explicit and transparent discussion of which components underperform (e.g., the failure of VQ-VAE codebooks for low-rank action spaces, or the inefficiencies of tight layer-by-layer VLM connections) adds significant value, saving the community from retreading failed paths.
- **Architectural Nuance:** The identification of the "soft strategy" (utilizing learnable queries as a latent buffer) as the optimal bridge between the VLM and the policy module effectively balances the expressiveness of tight coupling with the efficiency of loose coupling.
- **Efficiency:** The final VLANeXt model proves that careful, evidence-backed design choices can bridge the performance gap left by massive foundation models, offering a lightweight and highly competitive open-source baseline.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Area Chair Verification Risk (Anonymity):** The submission includes non-anonymized links to a project website and a GitHub repository in the preamble and footnotes. This is a direct violation of double-blind review policies, and the Area Chair must determine if this constitutes grounds for an administrative desk rejection according to venue guidelines.
- **Path-Dependent Findings:** The linear nature of the ablation study leaves the robustness of the 12 key findings vulnerable to the specific order in which the architectural changes were applied.
- **Visual Presentation Artifacts:** Figures 3 and 4 suffer from severe PDF text rendering and layering artifacts (e.g., duplicated and overlapping text such as "flowsflowsflows" or "MLLMMLLM"), which currently obscure critical architectural diagrams and detract from visual clarity.
- **Mathematical Ambiguity:** The description of the highly successful "soft" VLM-policy connection is currently too qualitative ("inserts learnable queries as a latent buffer"). Without formal mathematical definitions or specific dimension details in the main text, full independent reproducibility is hindered.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
- **Orthogonal Ablations:** To mitigate concerns regarding path dependence, the authors should provide at least one or two orthogonal ablations (e.g., applying the flow-matching objective or the soft VLM connection to an independently strong, distinct baseline architecture) to prove that the performance gains are independent of the specific evolutionary sequence shown in Figure 2.
- **Real-World Detailing:** A thorough elaboration of the real-world experimental setup is needed, including the number of physical tasks, trial counts, environmental noise profiles, and explicit confirmation of whether the relative performance trends observed in the simulation ablations hold true on physical hardware.
- **Formalization of the Soft Connection:** The inclusion of explicit equations defining the learnable query buffer and how it integrates the latent representations between the VLM and the policy head.
