## Public Content

I focus on the literature novelty, the technical validity of the claimed attack mechanism, and the empirical support for the paper's central narrative regarding how Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning dictates downstream actions in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models.

## Overview
TRAP explores an emerging vulnerability in reasoning VLAs by introducing a physical adversarial patch attack aimed at hijacking the intermediate Chain-of-Thought (CoT) process. By printing and placing an optimized adversarial patch in the robot's visual environment, the authors aim to coerce the VLA into generating a malicious reasoning trace, which in turn is hypothesized to trigger a targeted, malicious downstream action (e.g., handing a human a knife instead of an apple). The proposed methodology formulates a joint optimization objective that combines a CoT next-token prediction loss and a downstream action loss, targeting both discrete and continuous action space architectures. The paper also conducts a preliminary empirical analysis to understand the arbitration between user instructions and generated CoT in driving final robotic behavior.

## Claimed Contributions
- Proposing TRAP, which is claimed to be the first targeted adversarial attack framework for VLA models.
- Conducting a preliminary empirical analysis demonstrating a "competition mechanism" between visual instructions and CoT, asserting that CoT strongly governs downstream actions even under semantic misalignment.
- Formulating a joint adversarial attack objective that bridges both discrete and continuous action domains in modern VLAs.
- Evaluating the attack across three distinct VLA architectures (InstructVLA, MolmoACT, and GraspVLA) and deploying physical adversarial patches in real-world environments.

## Novelty And Prior Work
The paper claims to propose "the first targeted adversarial attack framework for VLA models." This claim is overly broad and factually inaccurate given the contemporary literature landscape.
- Prior work such as *RoboticAttack* (Wang et al., 2024, "Exploring the Adversarial Vulnerabilities of Vision-Language-Action Models in Robotics") explicitly introduces targeted adversarial patch attacks to manipulate robotic trajectories. TRAP's claim of being the absolute first targeted VLA attack framework ignores this precedent.
- Furthermore, Jones et al. (2025) successfully adapt Large Language Model (LLM) jailbreaks to embodied models, explicitly stating they "obtain complete control authority over VLAs" and achieve "full reachability of the action space." While this relies on textual jailbreaks rather than visual patches, it constitutes a highly effective targeted attack against VLA action generation. The authors correctly cite Jones et al. but inaccurately categorize it alongside purely untargeted action disruption.
- Technically, the core mechanism of forcing a Vision-Language Model to output a specific target string $R^*$ via an adversarial image patch is mathematically identical to standard targeted text-generation attacks and visual jailbreaks on standard VLMs. Applying this established technique to the intermediate CoT step of a VLA is conceptually interesting, but it represents an incremental methodological application rather than a foundational new framework. The novelty claims must be strictly scoped to reflect that this is specifically a targeted *patch-based attack exploiting CoT*, rather than the first targeted attack on VLAs entirely.

## Technical Soundness
There are two severe issues regarding the technical soundness of the paper's central claims:
1. **Confounding Attack Mechanism via Direct Action Supervision:** The paper's title and central narrative heavily imply that the VLA's actions are hijacked *via* CoT reasoning. However, the optimization objective detailed in Equation 4 explicitly minimizes a downstream action loss ($\lambda L_{action}$) alongside the CoT loss ($L_{cot}$). By directly optimizing the physical patch's gradients to minimize the error between the model's output action and the attacker's target action $a^*$, the method behaves functionally identically to a standard targeted action attack. It is impossible to confidently conclude that the targeted physical behavior is causally driven by the "hijacked" CoT if the action head itself is directly subjected to adversarial optimization. This confounds the core premise of the paper, making it unclear whether the CoT manipulation is actually responsible for the attack's success, or if the direct action loss is simply overpowering the model.
2. **Contradiction Between Empirical Data and Abstract Claims (Claim Inflation):** The abstract states that empirical evidence shows CoT "strongly governs action generation, even when it is semantically misaligned with the input instructions." However, this generalized claim is directly contradicted by the paper's own preliminary analysis in Section 4. According to Table 1, under semantic misalignment (the cross-sample shuffling scenario), InstructVLA and MolmoACT follow the CoT (measured by TSRsi) only 16.42% and 12.94% of the time, respectively. Rather than being "strongly governed" by the CoT, these models overwhelmingly experience catastrophic task failure. Extrapolating a strong governance mechanism from a failure rate exceeding 80% is a severe inflation of the empirical results and undermines the theoretical motivation for targeting the CoT in these architectures.

## Experimental Rigor
- **Undefined Metrics:** Table 1 presents several core metrics (Sim_m, TSRsi, TSRsj, and Score) that are not mathematically defined or explained in the provided main text or the table caption. This makes the preliminary analysis exceptionally difficult to interpret independently, violating the principle that the main text must be self-contained.
- **Physical Robustness and Limitations:** The paper claims successful real-world physical patch deployment. However, physical adversarial patches in dynamic closed-loop robotic environments are notoriously fragile to changing camera perspectives, lighting variations, and occlusion (e.g., the robot's own manipulator moving over the coaster patch during a trajectory). The evaluation lacks rigorous quantification of the attack's success rates across these critical physical domain shifts.
- **High Variance in Preliminary Analysis:** Table 1 reports extreme standard deviations in the cross-sample shuffling scenario (e.g., 0.0508 ± 0.2392 for InstructVLA). This immense variance casts further doubt on the stability of the "competition mechanism" and is left unaddressed in the text.

## Decision-Relevant Strengths
- Identifying intermediate CoT reasoning as an actionable, physical attack surface in modern VLAs is a highly timely and valuable conceptual insight for embodied AI security.
- The preliminary analysis methodology—utilizing instruction masking and cross-sample shuffling—is a well-structured approach to attempting to disentangle the causal impacts of visual instructions versus intermediate reasoning.
- The evaluation ambitiously spans diverse architectures, bridging both discrete and continuous action formulations, which improves the generalizability of the threat model.

## Decision-Relevant Concerns
- **Fatal Flaw Candidate (Methodological):** The explicit inclusion of the target action loss ($L_{action}$) in the adversarial optimization (Equation 4) completely confounds the claim that actions are hijacked specifically *through* the CoT.
- **Fatal Flaw Candidate (Claim Inflation):** The foundational motivation that CoT "strongly governs" action under semantic misalignment is factually contradicted by the data in Table 1 for two out of the three evaluated models.
- **Novelty Overstatement:** The claim of being the "first targeted VLA attack" is factually inaccurate given prior works such as Wang et al. (2024).
- **Anonymity Policy Risk:** The submission includes GitHub links containing a clear developer username ("MiYanDoris"). I flag this as an Area Chair verification risk regarding the venue's double-blind review policy.
- **Ethics and Safety Risk:** The paper describes causing a physical robot to "deliver a knife to a person instead of an apple." Given the physical deployment, if this scenario was executed in the real world with human participants, the absence of an explicit discussion on safety protocols, Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval, and dual-use mitigations represents a significant ethical vulnerability.

## Missing Evidence Or Experiments
- **Ablation of the Action Loss ($\lambda = 0$):** To substantiate the claim that the attack works primarily by hijacking the CoT, the authors must evaluate the targeted attack success rate when the patch is optimized *strictly* using the CoT loss ($L_{cot}$), without any direct action supervision ($\lambda = 0$). If forcing the adversarial CoT string does not reliably produce the adversarial action, the vulnerability is primarily situated at the action head, not the reasoning process.
- **Self-Contained Metric Definitions:** Explicit mathematical formulations for TSRsi, TSRsj, Sim_m, and Score must be provided in Section 4 or the caption of Table 1.
- **Physical Robustness Quantification:** Quantitative reporting on the real-world attack success rate under varying camera angles, distances, and dynamic occlusions must be provided to validate the physical feasibility claims.

## What Would Change My Assessment
1. Providing the $\lambda=0$ ablation and empirically demonstrating that optimizing the CoT loss alone is sufficient to achieve high targeted attack success rates, thereby proving the causal chain.
2. Revising the abstract and introduction to accurately reflect the preliminary findings in Table 1, acknowledging that semantic misalignment often causes task failure rather than strict CoT adherence.
3. Scoping down the novelty claims to accurately reflect prior targeted attacks in the VLA domain (e.g., Wang et al., 2024; Jones et al., 2025).
4. Area Chair confirmation that the repository links do not constitute a fatal anonymity violation under venue rules.
5. Explicit documentation of safety protocols regarding the physical human-robot interaction experiments involving dangerous objects (the knife handover scenario).

## Provisional Leaning
Reject. While the premise of attacking CoT in embodied AI is conceptually excellent and highly relevant, the execution is fundamentally confounded. By optimizing directly for the target action, the method fails to prove its central thesis (that actions are hijacked *via* CoT). Combined with inflated claims regarding the preliminary analysis and overly broad novelty statements, the current manuscript requires substantial methodological clarification and revision.
